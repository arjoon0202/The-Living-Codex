## Caste System

## Swarm Classes

The Mure Mure no Mi produces a diverse taxonomy of insect castes, each bred for different functions within the colony. As the user's command language and training refine, these castes grow more specialized and synergistic.

### Worker Class
*Reconnaissance*

Small, numerous, and tireless. The backbone of the swarm — used for reconnaissance, mapping, marking targets, courier work, and low-level stamina drain. Cute, honeybee-like in appearance. The scribes of the Codex.

### Soldier Class
*Combat*

Heavier combat variants with hornet and beetle-like features. Built for direct pressure, body shielding, interception, and formation fighting. They hold the line and execute doctrine.

### Herculean Class
*Heavy Assault*

Dense, extremely strong micro-bruisers. Fewer in number but absurdly powerful — used to reinforce strikes, carry weight, pin targets, or form living armor. The "how is that tiny thing carrying a cannonball?" caste.

### Venom Class
*Toxin*

Stingers and dust carriers specialized for toxins, paralytics, irritants, hallucinogens, and controlled lethality. The silent executioners of the Codex.

### Pollen Class
*Utility*

Utility insects that spread adhesive pollen, sleep dust, tracking powder, sensory markers, explosive pollen, or environmental interference. Where beauty meets battlefield control.

### Pheromone Class
*Manipulation*

Battlefield control specialists that manipulate attention, aggression, fear, cohesion, and coordination. They agitate enemies, induce unease, mask presence, and synchronize the swarm itself.

### Incense Class
*Aroma*

Aesthetic and strategic variants releasing sweet, calming, disorienting, or concealment-oriented scents. Sedative vapors, focus-enhancing aromas, and fragrant haze concealment.

### Chrysalis Class
*Adaptation*

Experimental evolution lines designed to test new resistances, survival traits, and specialist adaptations. Each line is a version update — the R&D branch of the colony.

### Elemental Class — Jellyfish Subset
*Elemental*

Small, luminous, jellyfish-like floaters — cute and ethereal, yet devastatingly versatile. Born from the principle that insects adapt to any environment, these units weaponize the elements: electrical discharge, thermal burst, cryo-pulse, concussive wave, and more. Beautiful and terrifying in equal measure.

### Bombardier Class
*Siege*

Siege-specialist insects modeled after bombardier beetles — compact, armored, and chemically volatile. Each unit carries twin reaction chambers that mix superheated compounds on command, producing targeted detonations ranging from concussive breaching charges to incendiary cluster bursts. Deployed in formation, they become a living artillery line. Alone, they are demolition sappers — precise, patient, and catastrophically effective. The Marshal considers them the Court's answer to fortified positions: walls do not stop the swarm. The Bombardiers ensure they never have to.

### Steel Council
*Fortification*

The heaviest caste in the swarm — ironclad constructs with exoskeletons hardened to rival seastone composites. The Steel Council does not charge, does not pursue, does not break formation. They *hold*. Arranged in phalanx geometries, they form living barricades that absorb cannon fire, Haki-infused strikes, and Devil Fruit attacks while the colony repositions behind them. Each member of the Council links its shell to its neighbors through interlocking mandibles, creating a seamless wall of chitin and will. They are the swarm's final answer to overwhelming force: you do not go through the Steel Council. You go around, or you don't go at all.
