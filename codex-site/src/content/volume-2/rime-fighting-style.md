## The Smallest Fist

*Pencak Silat ・ Jeet Kune Do ・ Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu ・ Wano Ryou*

Rime's pure fighting style is built for a single devastating truth: **the smallest combatant in the room is the most dangerous one in it**. His martial foundation draws from Pencak Silat — an art designed for smaller fighters to dismantle larger opponents through structural destruction, angular footwork, and ruthless joint targeting — Jeet Kune Do's philosophy of directness, economy of motion, and using whatever works — and Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu's ground-fighting supremacy, where leverage and technique defeat raw size. His Wano-lineage Ryou is the engine that makes it all lethal: every touch, every grab, every strike carries the potential for internal devastation.

In melee combat without his Logia powers, Rime fights from the **Harimau (Tiger) stance** — a low, crouching position adapted for his four-legged body that allows explosive upward launches and ground-level evasion. He uses the opponent's body as terrain: running up limbs, leaping from shoulders, striking pressure points from angles that bipedal fighters cannot reach. His Silat training manifests as brutal efficiency — every contact is both defense and offense, every deflection is a strike, every limb he touches takes damage. His Jeet Kune Do influence keeps everything formless: he adapts mid-fight, discards what doesn't work, and strikes by the shortest path available.

His Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is the art that weaponizes his greatest anatomical advantage: **a body designed for wrapping**. Where human grapplers use two arms and two legs, Rime uses four paws, a prehensile tail, and a flexible spine that allows him to coil around limbs like a living vice. His BJJ is not sport grappling — it is positional dominance turned lethal by Ryou. He establishes control, isolates a joint or a neck, and then floods the trapped structure with internal destruction. Opponents who have survived Rime's striking learn to keep him at distance. Opponents who have survived his grappling learn that distance was the only thing keeping them alive.

His Ryou transforms what would be annoying paw-swipes from a squirrel into devastating internal strikes. A tap on the wrist shatters the bones beneath the skin. A tail-whip to the knee destroys the joint from the inside. His entire fighting philosophy can be summarized in one sentence: **touch once, break everything**. Only three of his named techniques incorporate Electro. The rest are pure body, pure Haki, pure martial art — and they are more than enough.

## Rime's Melee Arsenal

### Sapu Bawah — Root Cutter
*Pencak Silat*
シラット・足払い・根断ち — Shiratto: Sapu Bawah — Nedachi

A low sweeping strike targeting the opponent's ankle tendons and Achilles — Silat's foundational takedown adapted for a squirrel's low center of gravity. Rime launches from all fours in a spinning ground-level arc, whipping his Ryou-hardened tail across the back of the ankle joint while simultaneously driving his rear paws into the opponent's planted foot. The tail delivers internal structural damage through Ryou; the paw strike destabilizes the base. Opponents with perfect guards at head and torso height discover that nothing is protecting the six inches of ankle between their stance and the ground. Rime can chain this into three consecutive sweeps across different targets without leaving ground level.

### Kuncian — The Iron Lock
*Pencak Silat*
シラット・鉄鎖 — Shiratto: Kuncian — Tessa

Silat's joint-locking principle applied through a squirrel's body. Rime leaps onto an extended limb — typically an arm mid-punch or a leg mid-kick — and wraps his body around the joint: forepaws on one side, hind paws on the other, tail coiled for leverage. Then he applies Ryou through every point of contact simultaneously, flooding the joint with destructive internal force while physically hyperextending it. The lock lasts barely a second — Rime is too small to maintain a grapple against anyone who tries to shake him off — but a second of awakened Ryou applied directly to an elbow or knee joint is enough to compromise the limb permanently. He releases and Soru-bursts away before the opponent can react, leaving behind a limb that no longer bends the way it should.

### Langkah Tiga — Three-Point Collapse
*Pencak Silat*
シラット・三歩崩し — Shiratto: Langkah Tiga — Sanpo Kuzushi

Silat's triangular footwork pattern elevated to a destruction sequence. Rime executes three rapid positional changes around the opponent — forming a triangle of attack angles — and delivers a Ryou strike at each point: first to the lead knee (structural compromise), second to the floating ribs (organ disruption), third to the base of the skull (nervous system shock). Each strike uses a different part of Rime's body — paw, tail, headbutt — and each arrives from a blind angle created by the previous positional shift. The three impacts occur within a single heartbeat. The opponent's body receives three simultaneous system failures from three different vectors, and the structural collapse begins before they understand they've been hit once, let alone three times.

### Intercepting Paw
*Jeet Kune Do*
截拳道・迎撃掌 — Sekkendō: Geigeki Shō

The purest expression of JKD philosophy in Rime's arsenal: the fastest path between two points is a straight line, and the best defense is hitting them before they hit you. When an opponent initiates an attack, Rime doesn't evade or block — he launches directly into the attack with a single Ryou-loaded paw strike aimed at the nearest vulnerable point. The opponent's fist is coming toward him; Rime's paw meets the inside of their wrist before the fist arrives, and the Ryou pulse shatters the carpal bones on contact. The "intercepting" isn't a counter — it is an attack that happens to arrive before theirs does. Economy of motion. Directness. Bruce Lee's central thesis, executed by a squirrel with the most refined Ryou in the world.

### One-Inch Devastation
*Jeet Kune Do*
截拳道・一寸破壊 — Sekkendō: Issun Hakai

JKD's famous one-inch punch reimagined through awakened Ryou. Rime places a single paw flat against the opponent's body — no distance, no wind-up, no visible preparation — and delivers a Ryou projection that detonates internally at whatever depth he chooses. The physical contact is gentle. A paw-pad pressed against armor, skin, or clothing. The internal result is catastrophic: a shockwave that originates at the point of contact and expands outward through the target's body, shattering, rupturing, or disrupting everything in its path. The technique requires absolute calm and absolute focus — which is why Rime only uses it in the moments when his chattiness stops and his star-signature contracts. When Rime goes quiet and puts a paw on you, the fight is over.

### Acorn Fist — Shattered Shell
*Ryou Art*
流桜術・団栗拳・殻割り — Ryūō-jutsu: Donguri Ken — Karawari

Rime grips an acorn in his forepaw and punches with it — the acorn serving as a focus point for compressed Ryou. The nut's natural hardness becomes a channeling medium: the Ryou enters the acorn, compresses within its shell, and on impact releases in a concentrated burst that is denser and more focused than an open-paw strike. The acorn itself shatters on contact, adding physical shrapnel to the Ryou detonation. Rime carries dozens of acorns specifically for this purpose — each one a single-use amplifier for his Haki. Against armored opponents, the focused Ryou penetration outperforms his broad-contact palm strikes, boring through defensive layers like a drill through wood.

### Wano's Whip — Tail Severance
*Ryou Art*
流桜術・和の鞭・尾断 — Ryūō-jutsu: Wa no Muchi — Bidan

Rime channels concentrated Ryou along the full length of his tail and whips it in a single cutting arc. The tail itself is Armament-hardened into a razor line, and the Ryou projection extends the cutting force beyond the physical length of the tail by several inches — an invisible blade that follows the visible one. Against flesh, the tail-whip opens deep lacerations. Against armor, the Ryou projection severs internal fastenings and structural supports. Against Haki-defended opponents, the concentrated linear Ryou application outperforms broad-area attacks by focusing all destructive force along a single cutting plane. Rime's tail is the weapon most opponents forget about. It is the one they remember longest.

### Kilat Tangan — Lightning Grasp
*Electro-Silat*
雷シラット・稲妻掴み — Rai Shiratto: Inazuma Tsukami

One of three techniques where Rime permits himself Electro. He lunges onto the opponent's body — arm, leg, torso — grips with all four paws, and channels maximum Mink Electro through every point of contact simultaneously while Ryou locks the opponent's muscles in place. The combination is devastating: Ryou prevents the body from flinching away, and Electro ravages the nervous system while the muscles are frozen. The technique lasts less than a second — Rime leaps clear before the opponent's involuntary electrical discharge can harm him — but the neural damage from even brief exposure is enough to compromise reaction time and coordination for the remainder of the fight.

### Spark Step
*Electro-JKD*
雷截拳・火花歩 — Rai Sekken: Hibana Ho

Rime channels Electro through his paw-pads while executing rapid Soru-burst footwork — each step leaving a charged residue on whatever surface he touches. Within seconds, the ground around the opponent is laced with dormant electrical charges connected by Rime's Ryou-threaded pathways. When Rime triggers the circuit — a snap of Electro from any distance — the entire web detonates simultaneously, creating a cage of lightning arcs across the floor that paralyzes anything standing within it. A JKD trap philosophy applied through Mink biology: use every step to set up the one that matters.

### Guarda de Esquilo — Squirrel Guard
*Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu*
柔術・栗鼠の構え — Jūjutsu: Risu no Kamae

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu's closed guard reimagined for a four-limbed squirrel with a prehensile tail. Where human grapplers use their legs to control an opponent's torso, Rime uses his entire body to control a single limb — wrapping forepaws above the joint, hind paws below it, and his tail coiled around the wrist or ankle for rotational control. The position gives him five points of contact on one limb, each one a potential Ryou delivery point. From Squirrel Guard, Rime controls the pace of the grapple: he can transition to submissions, sweeps, or simply hold position while his Ryou does the quiet work of dismantling the joint from the inside. BJJ's fundamental principle — position before submission — is his patience made lethal. He doesn't rush the finish. He secures the hold, feels the architecture of the joint beneath his paws, and then chooses exactly how to break it. Opponents who try to shake him off discover that a squirrel in full guard has more grip strength per body weight than any creature alive, and that every attempt to escape only deepens the control.

### Triângulo Fantasma — Ghost Triangle
*Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu ・ Ryou Art*
柔術流桜・幽霊三角 — Jūjutsu Ryūō: Yūrei Sankaku

Rime's most dangerous submission — a triangle choke that the opponent doesn't feel until it's locked. He begins from Squirrel Guard or any position where his tail is free, threading it around the opponent's neck in what appears to be incidental contact — a brush, a shift of weight, the kind of tail movement a squirrel makes a hundred times a day. His hind paws control the opponent's arm, creating the classic triangle geometry: neck compressed on one side by the tail, on the other by the trapped arm, with Rime's body forming the closing edge. The "ghost" is the Ryou component — rather than relying purely on mechanical blood-flow restriction, Rime projects Ryou through his tail directly into the carotid pathway, disrupting the energy flow to the brain before the physical choke fully tightens. The opponent's vision darkens, their thoughts scatter, and they reach for their neck to find a squirrel tail already locked in a figure-four behind their head. The technique was inspired by Kuncian — his Silat joint lock — but evolved through dedicated BJJ study into something more refined: not the explosive destruction of a lock, but the patient, inescapable tightening of a constrictor. Rime named it in Portuguese because, he claims, "the language sounds cooler for grappling." He is not wrong.

### Harimau's Crown — Electro Ryou Cascade
*Composite Art — Ultimate*
虎冠・雷流桜の連鎖 — Harimau no Kanmuri — Rai Ryūō no Rensa

Rime's ultimate melee technique — the convergence of Silat, JKD, Ryou, and Electro into a single devastating sequence. He launches from Harimau stance directly at the opponent, running up their body in a spiraling path — forepaws and hind paws striking pressure points in Silat's destruction sequence, tail whipping across joints with Ryou severance, every contact point leaving an Electro charge. At the apex — the opponent's head or highest point — he plants both forepaws on the crown and channels everything: full Ryou projection downward through the spine, maximum Electro discharge through the nervous system, Conqueror's Haki flooding the opponent's will with the absolute certainty that this small squirrel is the most dangerous thing they have ever touched. The entire sequence takes two seconds. The recovery takes considerably longer — Rime needs to be carried in Ajay's pocket afterward, which he insists is "tactical napping."
