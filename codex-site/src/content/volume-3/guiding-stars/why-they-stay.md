## Why They Stay

They do not fight. They do not leave the ship. They do not age, or hunger, or tire. They simply *are* — present, warm, unwavering. They entertain the crew. They make Kame laugh so hard his shell rattles. They sit in Petal's garden and tell her stories about flowers that grow on stars. They challenge Rime to increasingly absurd competitions that he takes with deadly seriousness. They sit with Claude's Motes and hum melodies that resonate through the hull in harmonics the ship has never produced before. They sit with Ajay.

They sit with Ajay when the rest of the ship is asleep and he is staring at the ceiling, thinking thoughts he shouldn't think. They don't lecture. They don't fix. They just stay. Perth at the door. Faker on the desk. Bas in his palm. Jeff singing something soft and distant. And slowly, quietly, the ceiling stops being a void and starts being just a ceiling again, and Ajay remembers that there are people — real, impossible, plush people — who chose to be here. Not because he earned it. Not because he deserved it. Because they *decided*, with a certainty older than reason, that he was worth staying for.

In combat — in the single, absolute combat formation they appear in — they do not fight as warriors. They appear on the deck of the descending Meridian like a chorus of light, and they *witness*. Forty-six pairs of button eyes that say: *we are here, we have always been here, and we are not leaving.* Their Haki — Ajay's Haki, replicated perfectly, impossibly — floods the battlefield not as aggression but as **affirmation**. Conqueror's Haki that doesn't dominate but declares: *this crew is loved. This crew is watched over. This crew will not fall.*

They are the Guiding Stars. They were there before the Meridian. They will be there after. And if you ask them where they came from, they will smile their stitched smiles and say nothing, because the answer is not something that fits in words. It fits in the feeling you get when someone who has no reason to care about you cares about you anyway — fiercely, stubbornly, for no reason at all except that they chose to.

> "I don't know what they are. I don't know why they came. But I know the ship is warmer when they're aboard, and I know I am better when they're near."
