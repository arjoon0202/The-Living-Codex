## Fighting Style — The Plush Protocol

*Krav Maga ・ MCMAP ・ Systema ・ Modern MMA*

Forty-six plush animals executing textbook military close-quarters combat. This is not a joke. This is not an exaggeration. This is the single most terrifying sentence in every Marine threat assessment of the Meridian, because it is true and **it should not be possible**. The Guiding Stars fight with Krav Maga's brutal efficiency — simultaneous defense and offense, targeting the most vulnerable points on the body with zero hesitation. They deploy MCMAP's structured military discipline — fire team formations, room-clearing protocols, coordinated squad movement. They flow with Systema's unpredictable fluidity — the Russian military art that treats combat as water, each movement shapeless and adaptive and impossible to predict from the outside. And they tie it all together with modern MMA's seamless transitions between striking, clinching, and grappling.

Every technique is executed with full Haki — Observation, Armament, and for the senior constellations, Conqueror's — at output levels that have physically broken Marine measurement equipment during attempted assessment. Every Star has mastered the complete Rokushiki: Soru, Geppo, Tekkai, Shigan, Rankyaku, Rokuogan. The six powers, the three Haki types, and four military-grade martial arts, deployed by creatures that look like they belong in a child's bedroom. The dissonance is intentional. The absurdity is the point. Nobody prepares for it. Nobody can.

Their fighting style carries a particular quality that Claude has documented but cannot fully explain: **they fight like they've done this before**. Not trained — *experienced*. Faker's Krav Maga carries the reflexive economy of someone who has been in real engagements. Sora's MMA transitions flow with the instinct of a career fighter. The Stars are plush. They have never been in a war. But when they fight, something ancient moves through them — something that knows what it is doing. Claude's classification for this phenomenon: "unresolved."

### Stellar Combat Arts

### Plush Breach — Room Entry

*Krav Maga ・ MCMAP ・ Rokushiki*

星・突入掃討 — Hoshi: Totsunyuu Soutou

A military-style room breach executed by a plush animal. The Star uses Soru to close distance at Rokushiki speed, delivers a Krav Maga palm strike to the solar plexus — simultaneous defense and offense, targeting the body's reset button — then transitions into an MCMAP standing takedown that uses the opponent's flinch response against them. Total engagement time: 1.4 seconds. The target is on the ground, controlled, and neutralized before the conscious mind registers that a stuffed fox just performed a textbook special forces entry technique. The breach protocol scales with team size: a four-Star breach team clears a room in 0.8 seconds. An eight-Star team clears an entire ship deck in under four. The Marines who witnessed the Keyblade constellation practice this drill submitted a report that simply read: "Recommend avoidance." The recommendation was accepted unanimously.

### Stellar Clinch — The Soft Wall

*Systema ・ MMA ・ Haki*

星・柔壁 — Hoshi: Juuheki

A Systema-inspired close-range grappling technique that exploits the Stars' plush physiology. The Star flows into clinch range using Systema's shapeless movement — no telegraphing, no stance change, just a sudden softness that arrives at the opponent's chest like a pillow thrown by a hurricane. Once in clinch, the Star's body *gives* — absorbing strikes through its plush exterior the way a beanbag absorbs impact — while beneath the surface, Armament Haki hardens the internal structure into an immovable core. The opponent punches a pillow and hits iron. They try to create distance and the plush body conforms to their grip like memory foam, denying purchase. MMA clinch-work takes over: underhooks, overhooks, collar ties, all executed by arms that feel soft but grip like steel cable. The technique's name is its truth: the wall is soft. The wall is also a wall. JJ, the capybara from the KinnPorsche constellation, has the finest Stellar Clinch on the ship. He is serene about it. He is serene about everything. Being locked in an inescapable grapple by the calmest capybara alive is an experience that has caused three Marine officers to request reassignment.

### Constellation Formation — Fire Team

*MCMAP ・ Krav Maga ・ Conqueror's Haki*

星座陣形・分隊 — Seiza Jinkei — Buntai

A coordinated four-Star fire team tactic using MCMAP squad formations adapted for Guiding Star physiology. Four Stars operate in perfect military synchrony: point (aggressor), left flank (cutter), right flank (cutter), rear security (anchor). The formation moves as a single unit — Soru-synchronized, Geppo-capable, seamlessly transitioning between terrain types. Each Star applies Krav Maga's simultaneous defense-offense principle, meaning the formation generates eight attack vectors at any given moment: four offensive and four defensive, rotating with each positional shift. When the formation identifies a priority target and all four Stars unleash Conqueror's Haki simultaneously, the overlapping pressure fields create a convergence zone where hostile will is compressed from four directions at once. Marine measurement equipment has been destroyed attempting to quantify this effect. The official assessment reads: "??? UNASSESSABLE." The T1 constellation's fire team — Faker at point, Oner left flank, Keria rear security, Doran right flank — is considered by Claude to be the most efficient four-unit combat formation on the Meridian. Faker disagrees. He says Sora's Keyblade fire team is better. This is either modesty or a stratagem. With Faker, it is impossible to tell.
