## The Embers — Rime-Resonant

Small, excitable soot-sprites and spark-touched beings that absorbed trace vapour-Electro energy from Rime's training sessions in the Crucible. They look like tiny soot-balls with bright orange eyes, miniature lantern-creatures that glow warm at their cores, and fuzzy coal-puffs that leave faint scorch marks on surfaces when excited. They are **chaotic, playful, and absolutely fearless** — inheriting every ounce of Rime's reckless courage and none of his actual combat ability, which makes them simultaneously inspiring and a fire hazard.

### 🔥 Hinoko — The Spark Ringleader

A soot-sprite with a single bright ember floating above her head like an antenna, permanently vibrating with barely contained excitement. Hinoko idolizes Rime and mimics everything he does — badly. She attempts Air Steps (she falls). She attempts Plasma Acorns (she produces a small pop and a wisp of smoke). She narrates her own attacks in a squeaky voice that is either endearing or maddening depending on who you ask. Rime pretends to be annoyed. Rime secretly watches her training attempts from around corners and has been caught smiling. He denies this with the conviction of a squirrel who is absolutely lying.

### 🔥 Susu — The Soot Trail

A soot-ball who leaves tiny footprints everywhere she goes — dark smudges on clean floors, countertops, Kame's freshly polished chassis, and occasionally on Ajay's doctrine journals (which he has stopped complaining about because the footprints are actually quite small and somewhat artistic). Susu is the official Crucible cleanup crew — a crew of one — and she takes the job seriously despite the fact that she is, by nature, the primary source of the mess she is cleaning. This irony is lost on her. It is not lost on Donguri, who has filed seventeen complaints.

### 🔥 Kagari — The Living Lantern

A lantern-creature whose inner core glows brighter during combat — pulsing from warm amber to blazing white as the crew's fighting intensity rises. During peacetime, Kagari serves as ambient lighting in the lower decks, drifting through corridors and casting a soft golden glow that makes the Meridian feel like home even in the darkest waters. During battle, she stations herself above the Crucible entrance and burns so bright that the entire deck is illuminated in emergency crimson. She is both nightlight and war-torch, and the transition between the two is the most accurate barometer of the ship's danger level that Claude has ever observed.

### 🔥 Kaseki — The Tiny Challenger

A coal-puff who claims to be the toughest being on the Meridian. He challenges everyone. Maru. Rivet. Petal. The ship's anchor. He squares up, puffs out his chest (which doubles his size from "tiny" to "slightly less tiny"), and charges. He loses adorably every single time — bouncing off targets, rolling away, and immediately getting back up to try again. The crew has a standing agreement to let Kaseki "win" at least once per week by dramatically falling over when he charges them. Rime, characteristically, refuses to participate in this charade and takes every challenge seriously, which means Kaseki has been plasma-flicked into walls more times than anyone can count. He keeps coming back. His spirit is genuinely unbreakable.

### 🔥 Homura — The Hearthkeeper

The calmest Ember — and the anomaly of the family. Where every other Ember is chaotic and loud, Homura is a tiny, steady flame-being who tends the ship's hearth with quiet devotion. She keeps the galley warm during cold-weather sailing, maintains the heating elements in the sleeping quarters, and ensures that the Meridian's common spaces always feel lived-in and welcoming. She rarely moves from the hearth, and the Roundlings have adopted her as an honorary member of their family because she embodies their domestic values while providing the one thing they cannot: warmth. She is proof that not every flame needs to be wild to matter.

### 🔥 Hibana — The Apologetic Arsonist

A spark-critter who accidentally sets small things on fire and immediately apologizes — bowing, squeaking in distress, and producing a tiny fire extinguisher from somewhere on her person (nobody knows where she keeps it) to frantically put out the blaze she just caused. Hibana has scorched napkins, singed curtains, ignited a stack of wanted posters, and once set Maru's apron on fire (an incident that nearly caused a diplomatic crisis between the Embers and Roundlings). She is deeply sorry. She is always deeply sorry. She cannot help it. Kame has fireproofed most of the ship's surfaces at this point, but Hibana finds a way. She always finds a way.

### 🔥 Kemuri — The Smoke Ghost

A smoke-wisp who is impossible to catch. Literally impossible. The crew has tried. The homies have tried. Rime — a creature capable of Observation Haki that tracks opponents across battlefields — has tried. Kemuri dissolves into thin air the instant anyone reaches for her and reconstitutes somewhere else entirely, usually behind the person who was trying to catch her. She loves hide-and-seek. She always wins. She has *never* been found during a game — not once, in the entire history of the ship. Claude, who is aware of every molecule aboard the Meridian, claims to know where Kemuri hides. Claude has declined to share this information, citing "the integrity of the game."

## Fighting Style — The Reckless Flame

*Hapkido ・ Judo ・ Taekkyon ・ Vale Tudo — Rime-Resonant Expression*

The Embers fight the way fire burns: **without plan, without restraint, and in every direction at once**. Their martial expression is dominated by Vale Tudo — the art of "anything goes" — because attempting to teach the Embers disciplined technique is like attempting to teach a bonfire to burn in a straight line. Hinoko has technically been trained in all four base arts. Hinoko uses whichever one she remembers in the moment, abandons it mid-technique if something more exciting presents itself, and occasionally invents entirely new forms by accident.

Their Taekkyon manifests as wild, rapid-fire kicks thrown from impossible angles — less "flowing dance" and more "small fire tornado with feet." Their Hapkido is present as an instinctive ability to redirect force, though the Embers express this by grabbing incoming attacks and setting them on fire rather than redirecting them gracefully. Their Judo exists primarily as a delivery mechanism: grab the opponent, cling to them, and raise core temperature until the opponent is forced to throw themselves just to escape the heat. Their Rime-Resonant Ryou is expressed as thermal amplification — every strike carries heat that burns from the inside, every touch leaves a thermal signature, and prolonged contact results in internal combustion.

Their Mantra is combat-tuned and chaotic — they read attack trajectories the way sparks read wind currents: instinctively, reactively, and with a gleeful disregard for what the information means beyond "dodge or don't, either way it'll be fun."

> "Analysis: fighting style unpredictable. Recommendation: don't." — Claude's Threat Assessment, Ember Family

### Ember Combat Arts

### Hinoko no Danmaku — Spark Barrage

*Vale Tudo ・ Taekkyon*

火の子の弾幕 — Hinoko no Danmaku

An all-out rapid-fire assault where an Ember launches kicks, headbutts, body-slams, and strikes in every direction with Taekkyon's speed and Vale Tudo's complete absence of rules. Each impact leaves a thermal Ryou charge on the target — a tiny ember of internal heat that builds with every subsequent hit. There is no pattern to read. There is no rhythm to predict. Observation Haki users describe trying to track the technique as "reading a book that's on fire." The barrage continues until the Ember runs out of energy or gets distracted by something shiny, whichever comes first. Hinoko holds the family record at forty-seven consecutive strikes. She broke it while narrating her own attack in the third person.

### Moetsuki — Clinch Ignition

*Judo ・ Ryou*

燃え尽き — Moetsuki

The Embers' grappling technique — and the reason experienced fighters never let an Ember grab them. Using Judo's grip-fighting principles, the Ember latches onto the opponent at any available contact point and immediately begins channeling Rime-Resonant thermal Ryou through every point of contact. Their body temperature skyrockets. The opponent's clothing begins to smoke. The skin beneath the grip reddens, blisters, burns. The Judo component keeps the grip locked despite the opponent's thrashing — each escape attempt only repositions the Ember to a new, equally devastating grip point. The technique's name means "burnout" — it continues until the Ember exhausts its thermal reserves or the opponent stops moving. Kagari, the Living Lantern, can sustain Clinch Ignition for ninety seconds. This is thirty seconds longer than anyone has ever needed.

### Kaseki no Chosen — The Tiny Challenger's Oath

*Hapkido ・ Vale Tudo*

化石の挑戦 — Kaseki no Chosen

Named for Kaseki, the coal-puff who challenges everything and everyone — but usable by any Ember who channels his defiant spirit. The technique is a last-stand counter: the Ember plants itself, channels every remaining ounce of thermal Ryou into a single point, and waits for the finishing blow. When the strike arrives, the Ember executes a Hapkido circular redirect — catching the attack, borrowing its force, and returning it as a single explosive counter-throw amplified by everything the Ember has left. The throw's heat is visible: the air shimmers, the thrown opponent trails flame, the point of impact glows. The technique fails more often than it succeeds. This is Kaseki's truth and the family's creed: *the failure rate is irrelevant because you get up and try again*. Kaseki has challenged Rime to single combat two hundred and thirty-one times. He has lost two hundred and thirty-one times. He will challenge Rime again tomorrow. Rime, for his part, has never once held back against him. This is the highest respect Rime knows how to give.
