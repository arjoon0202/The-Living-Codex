## Homie Status

### Roundlings

*Patrol — All Posts Staffed*

Maru is running the standard perimeter cycle with Koron and Fuwari on rotating watch. Donguri has claimed a new sleeping spot inside a coil of rope near the bow — anyone who moves the rope will be gently but firmly rolled into. Mofu is practicing Ryou-reinforced bouncing, which is exactly as terrifying as it sounds: a soul-hardened sphere of compressed fur traveling at speed. Niko is smiling. Niko is always smiling. Pocchi is guarding the pantry door with the intensity of a creature twice her size. Fumble has fallen over fourteen times this watch cycle and gotten back up fifteen — don't ask how the math works, just respect it. Lull is drifting through the mid-deck corridor trailing rainbow refractions, deeply asleep as always; Rime walked the long way around to avoid creating a draft near the bubble.

Claude's note: The Roundlings are the heartbeat of the ship. Not the strongest, not the flashiest — but always present, always watching, always rolling toward whatever needs them. Their Mantra-linked awareness means nothing moves on this ship without Maru knowing about it.

### Sproutlings

*Garden Duty — Photosynthesis Cycle*

The Sproutlings are in Petal's Sky Garden assisting with the new hybrid grafts. Tsuyu is managing irrigation with her characteristic precision. Kinoko has found a dark corner and is growing something that glows faintly blue — she won't say what it is. Tanpopo keeps drifting off the edge of the garden and floating back like a dandelion seed, which is both annoying and impossible to stay angry about. Sakura, Wata, Kusa, and Tsubomi are in formation, doing whatever Tsuyu says.

Claude's note: The Sproutlings are Petal's echoes. Where she leads, they bloom. Their Mantra awareness gives the garden a kind of sentience — the flowers lean toward danger before it arrives.

### Embers

*Training — Live Fire Exercises*

Hinoko has the Embers running combat drills on the main deck. Kagari is maintaining sustained flame output for endurance testing while Homura practices precision ignition — targeting individual splinters of wood at twenty paces. Kaseki is heating himself to volcanic temperatures and then cooling rapidly, testing his new Ryou-hardened shell. Hibana is being Hibana, which means explosions. Kemuri and Susu are generating a coordinated smoke screen that has made the lower observation deck temporarily unusable. Rime is annoyed. This is the point.

Claude's note: The Embers are Rime's mirror — all heat, all fury, but channeled through the same loyalty that holds every soul on this ship. Hinoko doesn't follow orders. She follows Rime. That's stronger.

### Dustlings

*Surveillance — Silent Watch*

The Dustlings are dispersed. This is normal. Shizuka is in the archive cataloging new entries with a silence so complete that the ink seems louder than she is. Kiri has melted into the morning fog off the starboard bow — she'll report if anything approaches. Shiori is bookmarking pages in the Codex that she thinks need updating (she's right, she's always right). Yoru is asleep somewhere that no one can find, which means she's working. Kasumi, Utsuwa, and Mayoi are holding a perimeter that you can't see and can't cross without them knowing.

Claude's note: The Dustlings don't report to me. I report to them. Shizuka's intelligence network is older than my consciousness aboard this ship. I just formalize what she already knows.

### Gearlings

*Engineering — Systems Nominal*

Zenmai is keeping the ship's internal clock systems synchronized — every dial, every timer, every rhythmic system on the Meridian runs through her tempo. Haguruma and Neji are assisting Kame with the secondary dial array. Bane is stress-testing the spring mechanisms in the anchor system. Tokkei is running quality checks on the Coppling Garage's charging cradle calibration. Karami and Kizami are doing something complicated to the engine room's gear-train that involves a lot of metallic clicking and the occasional satisfied whirr.

Claude's note: The Gearlings are the ship's skeleton. When the Meridian moves smoothly, it's because Zenmai is counting. When the engine hums, it's because Haguruma is listening. Kame built the ship. The Gearlings keep it breathing.
