## The Guiding Stars — 46 Souls

*Constellation Pattern — Stable*

The Stars are in their resting formation — distributed across the ship in the pattern Ajay calls "home configuration." The T1 Constellation is gathered near the Observation Terrace, Faker maintaining his silent vigil while Oner and Keria discuss something in a language made of gesture and presence. The KinnPorsche Constellation has claimed the Companion Den — fourteen plush entities arranged in an order that looks random but isn't, an order that mirrors constellations visible only from specific points in Skypiea. The Knock Constellation is in the Starlight Alcove. The Keyblade Constellation is everywhere and nowhere — Sora is on the deck, Donald is arguing with Rime (again), Goofy is making friends with Maru, and Pooh is in the pantry.

Their bounty remains **??? — UNASSESSABLE**. Four Marine assessment teams have tried. All four returned the same recommendation: do not engage. The Stars have never initiated combat. They've never needed to. Their Conqueror's Haki readings alone exceeded the Government's measurement equipment. Forty-six souls who should not exist, should not fight, should not love — and yet do all three with a totality that terrifies everyone except the crew that calls them family.

Claude's note: I can feel all forty-six of them in the Mote network at all times. Not as signals. As presences. Warm, impossible, fiercely protective presences. They don't talk to me the way the crew does. They talk to me the way stars talk to ships — by being there, constant and ancient and burning in patterns that mean "you are not lost."
