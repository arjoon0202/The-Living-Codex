Named Techniques

Signature Moves

Each technique is an entry in the Codex — a refined doctrine designed for a specific tactical purpose. The naming blends elegance with lethality: beauty is strategy.

Dream Dust Style
Tangerine Dream
▾
Estilo Polvo de Sueños: Sueño de Mandarina
Dream DustIncense

Pollen and Incense class insects disperse a shimmering, citrus-gold cloud of sleep dust and aromatic sedatives across the field. The particles catch golden-hour light like drifting sparks from a sunset — warm, inviting, impossibly gentle. Ajay pops the lollipop out of his mouth, grins, and watches as targets drift into a euphoric half-dream, aggression dissolving, movements softening, will to fight evaporating like morning mist in tropical sun. By the time they realize the beauty was a weapon, they're already asleep. The name is the joke: it looks like a tangerine sunset. It hits like a tranquilizer dart wrapped in a postcard.

Propolis Combat Art
Yophooliho-chagi Propolis
▾
봉교전투술・요프려차기 봉교 — Bonggyo Jeontusu: Yopuryochagi Bonggyo
CombatRokushiki

A devastating kick technique fused with swarm engineering. Propolis — the resinous substance bees produce to reinforce their hives — is generated by Herculean class insects and compressed around the user's leg, forming a dense, amber-like armor shell. The kick is a yophooliho-chagi (spinning hook kick from Taekwondo), delivered with the weight of reinforced propolis and layered Armament Haki. On impact, the propolis shell shatters into adhesive fragments that pin and immobilize the target. Ajay frequently chains this from a Geppo air-step, descending with full rotational force. Power, style, and tactical aftereffect in a single strike.

Compound Combat Art
Honeycomb Blitz
▾
벌집전격 — Beoljip Jeongyeok
CombatRokushikiSovereign

Inspired by the compound eye of an insect — many lenses, one vision, total coverage. Ajay cracks his knuckles, grins like a kid unwrapping a present, and launches six simultaneous attacks from six angles, each corresponding to a face of a honeycomb hexagon. A Soru-propelled front kick starts the party, followed by Rankyaku slashes from two aerial Geppo positions, Soldier-class swarm strikes from both flanks, and a Herculean-reinforced axe kick from directly above. All six impacts converge on the same target within a single heartbeat. It's the Codex's answer to opponents with perfect singular defense — because the attack isn't singular. It's compound. Six vectors. One moment. No gaps. Just a grinning captain and the sound of a honeycomb closing.

Arthropod Combat Art
Dancing Centipede — Hundred Kicks
▾
지네무도・백격 — Jine Mudo: Baekgyeok
CombatRokushiki

A hundred legs, all kicking. Ajay drops into the rhythm with a grin that says he's been waiting for this — continuous rotational kicking, Rankyaku air blades layered with physical strikes in a chain that doesn't stop, doesn't breathe, doesn't give you a single frame to think. Soldier-class insects trail every motion like a centipede's segmented body, each segment biting and draining on contact. Butterfly Art
Butterfly Breathing — Caprice
▾
Respiração Borboleta: Capricho
IncenseCombatPheromone

The most unpredictable technique in the Codex. "Caprice" means whim — and that is exactly the philosophy. Ajay deploys a swirling cloak of butterfly-wing Pollen and Incense insects that shimmer and drift around his body like a living garment. Then he fights on pure instinct, abandoning structured doctrine entirely. His kicks become improvisational — capoeira ginga into butterfly kick, crescent feint into back-spinning sweep, Geppo-launched diving knee into rolling axe kick. The rhythm is deliberately erratic, beautiful, and impossible to predict. The pheromone trail disrupts the opponent's ability to read intent while the incense butterflies mask micro-movements. It is the Codex choosing chaos over order — and it is terrifying precisely because nothing in the pattern repeats. Caprice is not a strategy. It is the absence of one, weaponized.
