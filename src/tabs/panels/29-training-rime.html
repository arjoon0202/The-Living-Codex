Breath of the Rodent Thunder God

BREATH OF THE RODENT THUNDER GOD — OMNI SHADOW CLONES OF THE MIST

How It Happened

Development

After the Shanks fight, Rime spent days brooding — which for Rime means pacing the Crucible and shocking everything within reach. He'd learned from Tidus about imbuing Haki into constructs to make them tangible and powerful. Combined with his own Logia awakening's vapour domain and Electro, he realized: if he could make the environment his body (Steam Country), he could partition fragments of that body into independent forms.

The breakthrough came when he noticed his vapour naturally condensed into different patterns based on the local electrical charge — and each pattern moved differently. He spent three weeks training in secret (well, "secret" — the Crucible was crackling with discharge and everyone on the ship could hear him laughing).

What emerged was something unprecedented.

齧歯雷神の息吹: 全方位霧影分身

Breath of the Rodent Thunder God: Omni Shadow Clones of the Mist

Gesshi Raijin no Ibuki: Zenhōi Kirikage Bunshin

Rime projects concentrated vapour-and-electricity constructs that take the form of different rodent species. Each clone is created from his Logia vapour body, imbued with his top-tier Ryou, Electro, and Conqueror's Haki — transforming them from intangible mirages into fully tangible combat entities capable of dealing real damage.

Each clone adopts a different rodent body with fighting styles adapted to their unique physiology. They are semi-autonomous — sharing Rime's battle instincts but making independent tactical decisions. Rime himself fights alongside them as the ninth combatant.

Eight bodies made of thunder and fog. One chinchilla made of bad decisions and worse odds. Nine reasons to run.

The Eight Clones

Rodent Thunder Corps

🐹 The Hamster — ハムスター

Golden-furred, round, crackling with static. Fights with devastating body-checks and rolling attacks. The heaviest hitter among the clones — uses its round body for Electro-charged cannonball attacks. What it lacks in finesse it makes up for in raw, tumbling, spherical destruction.

Hamster Clone
Thunder Ball
▾
雷球 — Raikyū

Curls into a dense ball and launches at targets with Soru-speed rotation, Electro arcing from every hair. The impact is less "punch" and more "getting hit by a bowling ball made of lightning that's personally offended by your existence." Walls, floors, people — the Hamster does not distinguish between obstacles. Everything is a surface to bounce off of toward the next target.

Hamster Clone
Cheek Burst
▾
頬爆 — Hōbaku

Stores compressed Electro in its cheek pouches and releases it as twin directional blasts at point-blank range. The charging phase looks adorable — puffy cheeks, round face, the picture of innocence. The release phase looks like twin flashbangs going off inside your guard. Hamsters store food in their cheeks. This one stores ten thousand volts.

🐿️ The Squirrel — リス

Agile, acrobatic, tail crackling like a lightning rod. The fastest clone — parkour-fights across any terrain using its tail as a conductive whip and balance pole. It doesn't run. It ricochets. Every surface is a launchpad, every angle an opportunity to arrive somewhere inconvenient for the target.

Squirrel Clone
Branch Lightning
▾
枝雷 — Eda Kaminari

Ricochets between surfaces at impossible angles, leaving lightning arcs between each contact point that form an electrified cage. The Squirrel touches the wall, the floor, the mast, the rail, the wall again — and between every point, a crackling line of Electro. By the time it stops moving, the target is standing inside a geometric cage of lightning with no safe direction to dodge. Rime calls this one "the cat's cradle." The Squirrel has never caught the joke.

Squirrel Clone
Tail Lash Discharge
▾
尾鞭放電 — Biben Hōden

Whips its electrified tail in a wide arc, the discharge traveling through the ground to strike targets at range. The tail itself is a Haki-hardened conductive whip — the crack of its swing ionizes the air in a line. But the real attack is underfoot: the current travels through whatever surface connects them, and the Squirrel always knows what's conductive.

😊 The Quokka — クアッカ

Perpetually smiling, radiating warmth. The tank — absorbs hits with a grin and returns them amplified. Its happy demeanor is genuinely unsettling to opponents. You can't read the combat intentions of something that's always smiling. Observation Haki returns nothing but contentment. That's the point.

Quokka Clone
Sunny Disposition
▾
陽気反撃 — Yōki Hangeki

Absorbs kinetic energy from incoming attacks and converts it to Electro charge, then releases it all in a single devastating counter-hug. The Quokka opens its arms. It takes the hit. It smiles wider. And then it hugs the attacker with every joule they just delivered, plus interest. The harder you hit the Quokka, the worse the hug. Marines have filed formal complaints about the psychological damage of being defeated by something that looked happy about it.

Quokka Clone
Happy Tremor
▾
幸福震動 — Kōfuku Shindō

Stomps the ground while grinning, sending Electro-charged seismic waves that disrupt footing and Haki concentration. The tremor doesn't just shake the ground — it shakes the target's focus. The frequency interferes with Haki flow, making it harder to maintain Armament or concentrate Observation. All while a small round rodent beams at you like you're the best thing it's ever seen.

🐭 The Mouse — ネズミ

Tiny, almost invisible, terrifyingly fast. The infiltrator — slips through guards and delivers precision Electro strikes to nerve clusters. Size IS the advantage. By the time you notice the Mouse, it has already been inside your guard for three seconds and made four contact points you haven't felt yet.

Mouse Clone
Whisker Acupuncture
▾
髭鍼術 — Hige Shinshijutsu

Touches specific nerve points with electrified whiskers, causing involuntary muscle spasms that break the target's stance. Each whisker carries a precisely calibrated micro-charge — not enough to cause visible damage, but enough to make the deltoid fire when the brain said "bicep," make the knee buckle when the intent was "step forward." The Mouse doesn't beat you. It makes your body disagree with your brain until the disagreement becomes fatal.

Mouse Clone
Silent Surge
▾
無音放流 — Muon Hōryū

Delivers a near-undetectable current through skin contact that short-circuits the nervous system's signal pathways. The target doesn't feel pain. They don't feel the Mouse. They feel nothing — and then their arm doesn't move when they tell it to. Then their leg. Then their diaphragm. The current is so subtle it slides under Observation Haki's detection threshold. Petal once described it as "the cruelest technique on the ship." Rime took it as a compliment.

🦔 The Porcupine — ヤマアラシ

Quills crackling with voltage, defensive nightmare. Each quill is a Haki-hardened lightning rod that can be launched as projectiles or erected as a defensive perimeter. Approaching the Porcupine is a decision you make once and regret immediately.

Porcupine Clone
Quill Storm
▾
針嵐 — Hariarashi

Launches all quills simultaneously, each one a Haki-hardened Electro-tipped projectile that homes in on bioelectric signatures. They regrow in seconds. The first volley clears the field. The second volley — arriving four seconds later from a full regrowth — catches anyone who thought the Porcupine was now defenseless. It is never defenseless. It is a vapour construct that grows lightning needles. The concept of "defenseless" does not apply.

Porcupine Clone
Lightning Urchin
▾
雷海胆 — Rai Uni

Erects all quills into a sphere of overlapping electrical fields. Anything that touches the sphere completes the circuit. Not recommended. The Porcupine rolls itself into a ball — quills out, voltage maxed — and becomes a stationary mine of absolute denial. Nothing gets in. Nothing wants to. Claude logged 340,000 volts per quill during testing and then quietly updated the Crucible's insurance classification from "training room" to "active hazard zone."

🐹 The Guinea Pig — モルモット

Stout, sturdy, surprisingly powerful. The grappler — uses its dense body and low center of gravity for wrestling holds amplified by Electro-conducted joint locks. Once the Guinea Pig gets its paws on you, the fight becomes a very unpleasant physics lesson about conductivity and leverage.

Guinea Pig Clone
Static Clinch
▾
静電締め — Seiden Jime

Grabs the opponent with all four limbs and channels continuous Electro through direct body contact. The more the target struggles, the stronger the charge cycles. Muscle contraction generates bioelectric signals; the Guinea Pig reads those signals and matches them with opposing currents. Fighting the hold makes the hold tighter. Relaxing into the hold means you've accepted being hugged by a lightning-wrapped rodent. There is no good option.

Guinea Pig Clone
Grounding Slam
▾
接地投げ — Setchi Nage

Lifts and throws the target into the ground while simultaneously driving an Electro current through them into the earth, using the ground as a discharge sink. The slam itself hurts. The discharge that follows hurts more. The current exits through every point of ground contact — back, shoulders, legs — turning the entire body into a conductor between the Guinea Pig's paws and the planet beneath. Tidus watched a test run and said, "That's just mean." Rime grinned for an hour.

🦫 The Capybara — カピバラ

Largest clone, calm demeanor, enormous power. The area controller — its sheer size and relaxed fighting style creates a zone of "you don't want to be near this thing" that controls battlefield positioning. It does not chase. It does not rush. It walks toward you, and you realize the space you're standing in belongs to it now.

Capybara Clone
Lazy Thunder
▾
怠雷 — Tairai

Generates a massive low-frequency Electro field that makes the air heavy and hard to breathe within a 30-meter radius. Just stands there. Looking relaxed. Being devastating. The field doesn't attack — it oppresses. Lungs work harder. Muscles fatigue faster. Haki concentration frays at the edges. The Capybara yawns occasionally. This is not a combat action. It is genuinely bored. The yawn generates a secondary pressure wave that makes the field worse. Rime did not plan this. He finds it hilarious.

Capybara Clone
Tidal Press
▾
潮圧 — Chōatsu

Walks forward. That's it. But every step sends Electro-charged pressure waves through the ground and air simultaneously, compressing the space the opponent can occupy until there is none. The Capybara does not accelerate. It does not need to. The pressure waves stack — each step adding to the last, the compression zone shrinking, the air getting thicker, the ground vibrating harder — until the target has been pushed into a space too small to fight in and too charged to escape from. The Capybara arrives. It looks down. It sits on you. This is the technique.

🪂 The Sugar Glider — フクロモモンガ

Aerial specialist with membrane-wings crackling with plasma. The air superiority clone — glides above the battlefield raining down electrical strikes from angles no one expects. It doesn't fly so much as surf the ionized air it creates, a tiny nightmare riding its own lightning like a kite made of bad news.

Sugar Glider Clone
Plasma Kite
▾
プラズマ凧 — Purazuma Tako

Spreads its membrane wings wide and generates a flat plasma disc beneath them, riding the ionized air like a surfboard while dropping concentrated lightning bolts from below. The bolts are small — pencil-thin — but Haki-hardened and precise enough to target the gap between helmet and collar, the joint of a knee guard, the exact spot where your Armament is thinnest. It fires from above at angles that make shields useless. Looking up to track it means taking your eyes off the seven ground-level nightmares closing in.

Sugar Glider Clone
Volt Dive
▾
急降圧 — Kyūkō Atsu

Folds wings and dives at terminal velocity, body becoming a plasma-wrapped missile. At the last moment, spreads wings to decelerate — dumping all the kinetic energy as a massive Electro discharge on impact. The dive is fast enough to leave a plasma trail in the air. The deceleration is violent enough to crack the ground in a ring. The discharge is powerful enough that Claude logs it separately from the other clone techniques in the damage assessment. The Sugar Glider uses this once per engagement. Once is enough.

齧歯雷神連撃: 八重雷閃乱打

The Combined Assault

Rodent Thunder God — Ultimate
Rodent Thunder God's Barrage: Eightfold Flash Rampage
▾
齧歯雷神連撃: 八重雷閃乱打 — Gesshi Raijin Rengeki: Yae Raisen Randa

All eight clones plus Rime himself — nine rodent bodies in total — execute a simultaneous multi-directional assault that defies tracking.

Each clone Soru-bursts from a different angle, their electrical signatures merging into a single strobing field that makes it impossible to distinguish individuals. The assault arrives as a storm of strikes from every direction at once — the Hamster crashing through the center, the Mouse targeting nerve clusters, the Porcupine launching quill-barrages, the Sugar Glider diving from above, the Squirrel ricocheting between them all linking lightning arcs, the Capybara controlling the exit routes, the Guinea Pig locking down movement, the Quokka absorbing and reflecting any counterattacks — while Rime himself delivers the final blow: a full-power Electro-Ryou strike channeled through all eight clones' electrical fields simultaneously.

Nine bodies. One thunderclap.

The target experiences every strike as a single, continuous, inescapable barrage of electricity and violence delivered from nine rodent-shaped natural disasters in the time it takes to blink. Observation Haki sees nine futures arriving simultaneously from nine directions and cannot prioritize. Armament Haki cannot harden everywhere at once. The exit routes are Capybara. The air belongs to the Sugar Glider. The ground belongs to everyone else.

Rime calls it "the best technique he's ever come up with." He's probably right.

Claude's Note: During the first full test in the Crucible, all eight clones manifested simultaneously for eleven seconds before destabilizing. Rime was unconscious for two hours afterward. He woke up, said "that was awesome," and immediately tried again. Current stable duration: forty-three seconds. He is training to extend it. The ship's lighting flickers every time he practices.
