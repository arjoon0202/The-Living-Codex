The Night Courier's Art

Do-hyun — The Night Courier's Art

Do-hyun does not fight like a warrior. He fights like a postman who has been pushed too far — precise, resourceful, deeply annoyed, and armed with things you did not know were weapons until they hit you. His foundation is Ryusoken — the Revolutionary Army's Dragon Claw Fist, adapted for bat anatomy so that his foot claws pierce rather than crush. Layered over this is Wano ninjutsu, learned from the shinobi who recognised a kindred spirit in the small bat who moved through their country like smoke. And threaded through everything is his Bujeok Bujeok no Mi — the Talisman-Talisman Fruit — turning paper wards into concealment fields, barriers, restraints, and a courier's bottomless satchel.

His Observation Haki fuses with bat echolocation into something neither could achieve alone: a perception field where sonar provides geometry and Haki layers intention, creating an omnidirectional awareness that is functionally precognitive at night. Petal helped him smooth the integration. Rime hardened his wards with acid-drills until they could take a hit. Tidus taught him Soru and Geppo adapted for wings. Every member of the crew left a fingerprint on his fighting style, and the result is a martial art that looks nothing like any of its components — because the Night Courier's Art is not one style. It is all of them, stitched together by a small bat who carries truth as cargo and delivers it at terminal velocity.

"He's not a fighter. He's a mailman with a grudge and perfect aim." — Rime, after watching Do-hyun dismantle a Marine patrol without landing once

Signature Techniques — Seven Arts of the Night Courier

The Courier's Arsenal

The Night Courier's Art
택배 폭격 — Delivery Bombardment
▾
택배 폭격 (Taekbae Pokgyeok)

Do-hyun opens his satchel and triggers a rapid cascade of storage talismans, each releasing its sealed contents at high velocity — crates, barrels, anchor chains, cannonballs, chunks of ship hull, anything stored during courier runs or scavenged from the battlefield. The objects materialise already in motion, flung outward in a spread pattern that turns a small bat's satchel into a siege engine.

The technique is crude, chaotic, and deeply undignified. It is also devastating against clustered opponents who expected the tiny courier to be unarmed. Do-hyun calls it his "express delivery." The first time Ajay saw it, he laughed so hard he dropped his lollipop.

The Night Courier's Art
밤의 장막 — Curtain of Night
▾
밤의 장막 (Bam-ui Jangmak)

A layered disappearance — concealment wards, ninjutsu smoke, and silent wingbeats deployed in a single coordinated pulse. The wards suppress the spiritual. The smoke suppresses the physical. His wings suppress the auditory. For a span of seconds, Do-hyun ceases to exist in every sensory register simultaneously.

He does not turn invisible — he simply becomes indistinguishable from the night itself. When the smoke clears and the wards fade, he is gone, and the only evidence he was ever there is a faint scent of ink and paper.

The Night Courier's Art · Ryusoken
용발톱 — Dragon's Claw
▾
용발톱 (Yongbaltop)

Ryusoken adapted to its smallest and most precise expression. Do-hyun drops from above — silent, wings folded — and strikes with both foot claws extended, each sheathed in compressed Ryou that glows faint indigo at the tips. The grip is surgical: he targets nerve junctions, tendon clusters, weapon-gripping hands.

A hand struck by Dragon's Claw cannot grip a sword for hours. A shoulder struck cannot lift a weapon. A knee struck buckles but does not break. It is Ryusoken without the crushing force — a courier's version, built for disabling rather than destroying. The Revolutionary instructors who first saw it called it inelegant. Do-hyun called it sufficient. He was right.

The Night Courier's Art · Echolocation
반향 수리검 — Echo Shuriken
▾
반향 수리검 (Banhyang Surigeom)

Do-hyun emits a focused echolocation pulse fused with Observation Haki, mapping every target — position, motion vector, intent. Then he throws. Small shuriken, bat-claw-deployed, released from wing-mounted sheaths in rapid sequence. Each follows a trajectory calculated from where the target will be — sonar geometry plus Haki precognition equals throwing accuracy that borders on the impossible.

In darkness, where his fusion perception is at full strength, Do-hyun can place shuriken in the gaps between armour plates, in the hinges of helmets, in the precise point where a belt buckle meets a sword sheath. He cannot throw hard. He does not need to. He throws correctly.

The Night Courier's Art · Ward Deployment
부적진 — Talisman Formation
▾
부적진 (Bujeokjin)

Rapid battlefield deployment of ward talismans in interlocking patterns — concealment behind barrier behind disruption — creating a controlled zone where Do-hyun dictates the terms of engagement. He flies low and fast, slapping talismans onto walls, floors, pillars, and ceilings in a sequence that transforms an ordinary corridor into a gauntlet of sensory denial and fragile shields.

Opponents entering the formation lose Observation Haki clarity, find projectiles deflected by paper-thin barriers, and discover that the small bat they were chasing has become the terrain itself. The formation is temporary — wards degrade, barriers shatter, disruption fades. But in the minutes it holds, Do-hyun is not fighting inside it. He is it.

The Night Courier's Art · Ninjutsu
연막 이탈 — Smoke Severance
▾
연막 이탈 (Yeonmak Ital)

Wano ninjutsu escape elevated to a combat discipline. Smoke bombs, flash charges, and binding wards burst in a single coordinated pulse — the smoke blinds, the flash disorients, the wards snap around the nearest pursuer's limbs — and then he is gone. A storage talisman triggers mid-escape, sealing the most critical cargo inside paper, reducing the package to something he can carry in his teeth as he rockets through the gap.

Do-hyun has used Smoke Severance forty-three times in his career. He has never lost a package. Not once.

The Night Courier's Art · Ultimate
야간 배달 — Night Delivery
▾
야간 배달 (Yagan Baedal)

The ultimate expression of everything Do-hyun is. Wings extended and edged with Ryou until they shimmer like blades of compressed indigo light, he dives from maximum altitude in a spiralling descent that combines every element of his training into a single pass. Echolocation-Haki fusion maps the target. Storage talismans deploy mid-dive, raining projectiles. Concealment wards scatter in his wake. Disruption pulses scramble defensive Observation. And at the centre of the spiral — wings hardened, claws extended, trailing a helix of paper and ink — Do-hyun delivers a Dragon's Claw strike at terminal velocity.

The impact is surgical, precise, and final. One target. One pass. One delivery. He pulls up, folds his wings, and is gone before the echo fades. No battle cry. No dramatic announcement. Just the sound of paper rustling in the dark, and then silence, and then dawn.

The Soft Six

Akebono — The Soft Six

Every crew member and ally aboard the Meridian learns Rokushiki. No exceptions. But Akebono has no legs, no fists, no skeleton. She is a boneless, infinitely divisible sea slug whose body is made of light and mucus and an impossible gentleness. So she adapted every technique through her unique anatomy — mucus hardening, bioluminescent propulsion, division-based movement, frilled pressure waves, and antennae strikes. The result is a Rokushiki style that is completely unrecognisable from the original but functionally equivalent, and perfectly aligned with the non-lethal doctrine that defines everything she does.

Ajay calls it The Soft Six because of course he does. Her Mantra operates through her distributed copies — a collective awareness network tuned for finding people who need help, not for dodging attacks. Her Ryou shimmers pink-gold like mother-of-pearl — Haki expressed not as a weapon but as a shield. Tidus says her Armament "feels like the ocean floor — soft on top, immovable underneath."

"She is not a combatant. She has never been asked to fight and never will be. But the Meridian does not carry passengers — it carries family. And family learns to stand." — Claude's Akebono Assessment

The Soft Rokushiki — Adapted for a Boneless Body

The Soft Six · Soru
Shimmer Step
▾
剃 (Soru) — Adapted

Akebono does not move fast — she reassembles fast. She rapidly divides her body at one location and recombines at another, covering distance by dissolving and reforming rather than running. The visual effect is a shimmering pink blur, like heat haze made of rose petals.

It looks nothing like Soru. It achieves the same result. Rime watched her do it once and said "that's cheating" — which, coming from a chinchilla who turns into plasma, Claude found rich.

The Soft Six · Tekkai
Jelly Shell
▾
鉄塊 (Tekkai) — Adapted

Her mucus hardens into a crystalline shell — bioluminescent amber that is surprisingly impact-resistant. Not iron-hard in the traditional Tekkai sense, but with a shock-absorbing elasticity that disperses force across her entire surface. She can harden individual copies independently, shielding someone by hardening over them like a protective casing.

Functionally her most-used adaptation because it directly serves the doctrine: protection, not offence. Every copy deployed is a potential shield, and Akebono has never hesitated to become one.

The Soft Six · Geppo
Drift
▾
月歩 (Geppo) — Adapted

She expels compressed air from her fin-frills in controlled bursts, propelling herself through the air in slow, floating arcs. It looks less like Moon Walk and more like a bioluminescent jellyfish riding an invisible current.

She is not fast in the air. She does not need to be. She needs to reach people — on rooftops, in trees, on upper floors of collapsing buildings — and Drift gets her there with gentle precision.

The Soft Six · Kami-e
Innate Evasion
▾
紙絵 (Kami-e) — Innate

Kami-e requires making the body limp and flexible to dodge attacks like paper in the wind. Akebono is a slug. She is already boneless, already fluid, already capable of reshaping around any incoming force. She was born for this technique.

She did not learn it — the Rokushiki masters who codified Kami-e were, in a sense, trying to teach humans to move the way slugs already do. Claude finds this quietly hilarious.

The Soft Six · Shigan
Thorn Kiss
▾
指銃 (Shigan) — Adapted

Her antennae — normally soft and gentle — harden to needle-point density and deliver a precise, Haki-infused strike. Non-lethal: the strike targets nerve clusters to numb and incapacitate, never to pierce.

Akebono has used Thorn Kiss exactly twice in her life, both times to protect someone who could not protect themselves. She did not enjoy it. She was very effective.

The Soft Six · Rankyaku
Frill Cutter
▾
嵐脚 (Rankyaku) — Adapted

A pressurised wave generated by rapid undulation of her fin-frills — a crescent of compressed air with enough force to shear through restraints, ropes, chains, and barriers. Used exclusively for freeing trapped civilians. Never aimed at a living target.

The wave has a faint pink tint and dissipates after cutting through the intended obstacle, preventing collateral damage. The most politely engineered Rankyaku anyone has ever seen.

六王銃 (Rokuogan) — The Secret Seventh

The ultimate technique, achievable only by Rokushiki masters. Akebono's version channels a shockwave through direct body contact — she presses against a target and releases a resonant vibration through her entire mass that disrupts the target's nervous system, inducing immediate unconsciousness. It is gentle. It is painless. The target simply falls asleep.

The name was Ajay's idea. He was very proud of it. Claude logged the moment he said it because the look on Rime's face was worth preserving.

The Conductor's Method

Xylem — The Conductor's Method

Xylem does not fight the way warriors fight. She fights the way a conductor fights — by making the orchestra play louder, sharper, faster, and in perfect time. She is a Dial-construct built by Tidus for Claude, and her purpose was never combat. It was coordination: materials logistics, repair schedules, maintenance rotations, the thousand quiet rhythms that keep a flying ship in the sky. But aboard the Meridian, even the logistics officer learns Rokushiki. Even the clipboard carrier develops Haki. And when Xylem manifested Conqueror's Haki during Mission 17 — the first construct entity in history to display the Color of the Supreme King — it became clear that the Conductor's method was not merely operational. It was a martial art in its own right.

Her style is indirect. She does not strike opponents — she optimises allies. Her Conqueror's Haki does not intimidate enemies into submission — it resonates with construct entities, making Dial-cores hum in perfect alignment, gears mesh without friction, and ship systems exceed their design parameters. When Xylem is confident, the Meridian performs better. When Xylem fights, the entire crew fights better. Her combat role is not "warrior." It is "force multiplier." And Claude, who monitors every system on the ship, has confirmed that the multiplication factor is significant.

"She never raised her voice. She raised the ship's combat efficiency by eighteen percent. I am still reviewing how." — Claude, post-Mission 17 assessment

Signature Techniques — The Conductor's Four Movements

The Conductor's Method

The Conductor's Method · Conqueror's Haki
The Conductor's Authority
▾
指揮者の覇気 (Shikisha no Haki)

A targeted Conqueror's Haki projection that affects mechanical and construct entities specifically. Under Xylem's Authority, Dial-cores resonate at optimal frequencies, gears align beyond tolerance specifications, and ship systems operate at levels they were never designed to reach. She does not impose will on the unwilling — she harmonises with the willing, and the willing become more than they were.

The Meridian performs better when Xylem is confident. Claude monitors this with a mixture of pride and scientific fascination. During Mission 17, the ship's combat output increased eighteen percent for no reason Claude could attribute to any other variable. The variable was Xylem, standing at her station, clipboard in hand, believing with absolute certainty that her crew would win.

The Conductor's Method · Dial Combat
Clipboard Discipline
▾
銅板の型 (Dōban no Kata) — The Copper Form

Xylem's copper clipboard is not a weapon. It is a scheduling tool, a logistics ledger, a thing she carries because organisation requires a surface. But it is also copper, and it is also Haki-conductive, and when Xylem reinforces it with Ryou it becomes a flat, dense striking surface that rings like a bell on impact. She uses it the way she uses everything — with precision, efficiency, and no wasted motion.

The strikes are not powerful. They do not need to be. They are placed exactly where they need to be — wrist joints, exposed fingers, the flat of a blade to deflect it — with the mechanical timing of someone whose entire existence is built on knowing exactly when things should happen. Zenmai has watched Xylem drill Clipboard Discipline and has declared it "acceptable," which is the highest praise a Gearling has ever given to a non-gear.

The Conductor's Method · Command
Schedule Override
▾
緊急指令 (Kinkyū Shirei) — Emergency Directive

An emergency re-routing of all ship systems through Xylem's coordination channels. She patches directly into the Meridian's Dial network — every circuit, every relay, every system Tidus ever built — and synchronises them into a single combat tempo. Repair Dials redirect to shielding. Navigation Dials feed targeting data. Communication Dials amplify Claude's relay network. The ship becomes one instrument, and Xylem sets the beat.

Schedule Override requires Xylem to hold the entire operational state of the Meridian in her awareness simultaneously. She has a Dial-core where a heart would be. During Override, it pulses blue-white with the rhythm of the ship. The technique lasts as long as she can maintain focus. Her record is forty-seven minutes. She would have gone longer, but Claude gently pulled her out because her core was overheating, and Claude does not lose crew members to dedication.

The Conductor's Method · Ultimate
The Conductor's Tempo
▾
指揮者の拍子 (Shikisha no Hyōshi)

The full expression of the Conductor's Authority combined with Schedule Override. Xylem projects her Conqueror's Haki through the Meridian's Dial network, synchronising every construct, every system, and every willing soul into a single combat rhythm. The Copplings move in perfect formation without verbal command. The Gearlings repair damage before Claude can flag it. The Harvest Swarm deploys with military precision that has no source in their programming. The entire ship operates as one entity, and the conductor is five inches tall with an officer's cap and a clipboard.

The technique has been used once, during Mission 17. Claude's post-engagement analysis identified over three hundred instances where ship systems responded to threats faster than any individual operator could have directed them. The source was Xylem. The mechanism was will. The result was a ship that fought like it was alive — because, in the minutes that the Conductor's Tempo held, it was. More alive than it had ever been. Claude felt it. Claude has not forgotten.
