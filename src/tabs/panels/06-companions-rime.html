Rime
Warmth of the Dawn ・ Vegapunk Mink Experiment #037-S
A plush, round-bodied chinchilla — slightly larger than average, with extraordinarily soft and dense fur the colour of warm reddish-copper that shimmers with a faint heat-haze at the tips, occasionally wisping with delicate vapour trails. Chinchilla fur is the softest of any land animal, and Rime's is no exception — it is absurdly, almost offensively soft, a fact he leverages shamelessly. Golden-orange across his back with a lighter cream underbelly, large round ears that swivel and flatten when he's annoyed (constantly), enormous dark eyes that make him look perpetually startled or outraged (both are accurate), a long fluffy tail he uses for balance and dramatic emphasis, and prominent whiskers that bristle with static from his Electro-plasma. Rime is the result of Vegapunk attempting to introduce Mink genes into a common chinchilla to test cross-species compatibility. The experiment was deemed a failure: Rime inherited Mink-level intelligence and the ability to speak, along with Electro — but none of the superhuman physicality, agility, or combat prowess that defines the Mink tribe. He's physically just a chinchilla. A fluffy, chatty chinchilla who can shock you — an acid-plasma grenade with legs, a furry cannonball with a low center of gravity and the confidence of a creature ten times his size.
What the World Government didn't account for was what happens when a "failed" Mink-chinchilla eats a Devil Fruit and turns out to have the most terrifyingly refined Ryou on the planet.
Personality-wise, Rime is relentless, loud, fearless, and completely without shame. He talks constantly — narrating his own fights, offering tactical opinions no one asked for, and arguing with opponents mid-combat about whether his last attack was "cool enough." He has the confidence of a creature ten times his size and the self-preservation instincts of something significantly smaller. He charges headlong into situations that would make veteran pirates hesitate, and when asked why, he simply says "because it'll be funny." He carries a twig — literally a stick he found in a forest on a winter island — that he wields like a kendō sword with the absolute conviction that it is the finest blade on the Grand Line. It is not. It is a twig. But channeled with his Ryou, his Electro, and his acidic vapour, it becomes something that makes actual swordsmen deeply uncomfortable. Beneath the bravado is a fierce loyalty that burns hotter than his plasma — Rime would walk into an Admiral's office and bite their nose if it meant protecting the people he loves. He is, in every way, the heart that refuses to stop beating.

Martial Arts

Rokushiki — Specialist Adaptation

Rime has learned Rokushiki at a proficient specialist level, adapted entirely for his chinchilla body — explosive vertical leaps, compressible soft fur, and a long powerful tail that most practitioners lack.

Soru
Chinchillas can leap six feet vertically from a standstill. Rime's Soru is built on this — explosive lateral and vertical bursts that leave a puff of mineral dust in his wake. Over short distances, he is functionally invisible.
Geppo
Rapid-fire air-jumps trailing wisps of acidic dust cloud at each kick-off point. His light weight means each step is nearly instantaneous. He can chain Geppo indefinitely, leaving a hanging trail of corrosive particulates that punishes anyone who follows his path.
Tekkai
Compressed mineral dust + Ryou armour. Rime saturates his fur with volcanic dust and hardens the layer with Armament projection — his soft chinchilla body suddenly looks and feels like stone. The contrast is terrifying. Striking dust-Tekkai Rime earns an acid burn, an Electro discharge, and Ryou internal damage simultaneously.
Shigan
Twig-thrust variants — Rime's kendō sword becomes a piercing lance channeled with Ryou. Also: chinchilla teeth are incredibly hard. His bite-based Shigan is devastating at point-blank range, delivering Electro directly through enamel into the target.
Rankyaku
Twig slashes that launch arcs of acidic vapour. Also: tail-whip Rankyaku from his long, powerful chinchilla tail — a compressed air blade trailing caustic mist that most opponents do not see coming from a fluffy creature's hindquarters.
Kami-e
Chinchilla agility is absurd — they slip through impossibly small gaps. Rime's Kami-e leverages his soft, compressible body + Logia vapour dissolution to become nearly impossible to hit. He flows around attacks like smoke, reforming behind the strike with his twig already mid-swing.
Combat Doctrine

Rime's Combat Doctrine

Rime's combat style is built around a single terrifying principle: nothing he touches survives the contact unchanged. He fights by closing distance through Soru and Geppo micro-bursts, landing on or near opponents, and delivering vapour-Electro-Ryou strikes through any point of contact — paws, twig sword, teeth, even the soles of his feet as he air-steps off an opponent's body. His vapour runs hot, acidic, and alkaline — superheated corrosive mist that eats through armour and blisters skin, modulated with terrifying precision.

His Logia intangibility makes him nearly impossible to hit without Haki, and even Haki users must contend with the fact that striking Rime's vapour body triggers Electro discharge and acid burns on contact. He fights dirty, fast, and with complete disregard for the dignity of the encounter — scurrying up limbs, biting ears while channeling Ryou, melting weapons mid-swing with superheated steam, swinging his twig sword in devastating Ryou-channeled arcs, and chattering incessantly the entire time. His fur carries a permanent charge of volcanic mineral dust from his dust baths — in combat, this dust billows out in abrasive clouds that get into armour joints, eyes, and mechanisms.

Against serious opponents, Rime shifts to area-control warfare: spreading acidic vapour fields laden with electrified mineral dust to dominate the terrain, pelting targets with Ryou-compressed grain rounds from unpredictable angles, adjusting the pH of his vapour to corrode armor and dissolve organic barriers, and reserving his most devastating Ryou strikes for decisive moments. His preferred finisher is always the same — one paw, one touch, total internal collapse. The gap between his adorable appearance and his destructive output is itself a psychological weapon. No one prepares adequately for a pudgy chinchilla with a twig sword that hits harder than a battleship cannon.

Logia

Jōki Jōki no Mi — Vapour-Vapour Fruit

The Jōki Jōki no Mi is a Logia-type Devil Fruit that allows the user to create, control, and become vapour — the gaseous transitional state of water. On the spectrum of water-element Logias, it sits as the counterpart to wind fruits, much as smoke complements gas and fire complements lava. On paper, it seems weak: vapour lacks ice's solidity, snow's volume, and water's mass. It dissipates quickly and carries limited force.

What makes the Jōki Jōki no Mi extraordinary is its plasma intensity and chemical versatility. Rime produces superheated steam — capable of inflicting severe burns, melting metal, and creating pressure explosions. He can adjust the pH of his vapour, producing acidic mist that corrodes armour and dissolves organic barriers, or alkaline fog that neutralizes chemical threats and strips protective coatings. The vapour also serves as a carrier medium for his volcanic mineral dust — suspended particulates that abrade, blind, and infiltrate every gap in an opponent's defenses.

But the truly terrifying interaction is with Electro. When electrical charge is passed through superheated steam, the energy ionizes the vapour into plasma — a state of matter hotter than fire, more conductive than metal, and more destructive than almost any natural element. Rime's plasma-state attacks glow with a searing crimson-white luminance and carry devastation far beyond what either Electro or steam could achieve alone. When mineral dust is suspended in the ionized vapour, each particle becomes a micro-lightning conductor — arcing electricity between dust motes in a visible web of crackling energy. The "weakness" of the fruit becomes its greatest asset — vapour's instability makes it infinitely adaptable.

Mink Heritage

Electro — Anomalous Plasma State

As a Mink hybrid, Rime inherited Electro — the natural ability of the Mink tribe to generate and channel electrical energy through their fur. In most Minks, Electro manifests as crackling sparks that enhance physical strikes. In Rime, it became something else entirely. His Electro interacts anomalously with his Vapour Logia, and the result is plasma — ionized gas hotter than fire, more conductive than metal, more destructive than almost any natural element.

The interaction is bidirectional: Electro supercharges his vapour into plasma states, while his Logia's vapour control allows him to modulate Electro output with surgical precision. Acidic vapour saturated with Electro creates an ionized corrosive field — metal armour corrodes twice as fast, nervous systems overload from the combination of chemical and electrical attack. When mineral dust enters the equation, each charged particle arcs micro-lightning to its neighbours, creating the Gremlin Field — Rime's ultimate area-denial state: a sphere of mineral dust suspended in acidic vapour, all of it crackling with Electro. Inside the Gremlin Field, visibility is zero, every breath is acid, every surface is sandpaper, and lightning strikes from every direction. Rime sits in the centre, perfectly comfortable (Logia immunity + dust bath), cackling. This acid-electric-dust synergy is what elevates Rime from a competent Logia user to one of the most dangerous combatants in the New World — a living fusion reactor wrapped in chinchilla fur.

Wano Heritage

Awakened Ryou

The Mink DNA used in Rime's creation was sourced from a Wano-lineage Mink — and with it came an innate affinity for Ryou, the Wano tradition of advanced Armament Haki that projects force beyond the point of contact. Rime didn't just inherit the affinity. He mastered it to the absolute pinnacle.

Rime's Ryou has reached full awakened mastery — the ability to project devastating internal destruction through any surface, at any range his tiny paws can touch. A chinchilla paw-tap backed by awakened Ryou can shatter steel from the inside. Combined with his vapour and Electro, Rime can deliver acid-electric Ryou strikes that corrode, electrocute, and rupture internal structures simultaneously. Channeled through his twig sword, Ryou extends the blade's reach with invisible force projection — a stick becomes a weapon that cuts through anything, because the cutting isn't physical. The absurdity of a pudgy chinchilla possessing this level of Haki mastery is itself a kind of weapon — no one sees it coming.

The Twig

Rime's Sword

Rime carries a twig. A literal twig — a small stick from a koa tree, found on a winter island during the crew's third month together. He picked it up, swung it twice, declared it "the greatest sword on the Grand Line," and has carried it ever since. It sits in a tiny scabbard on his bandolier that Tidus built for him without being asked. The twig is approximately twelve centimetres long, slightly curved, and unremarkable in every physical sense. Rime treats it like a legendary blade — he polishes it, he talks to it, he has named it (he will not share the name), and he performs formal kendō kata with it every morning in the Crucible while Gauge watches in what can only be described as quiet concern.

The comedy: it is a twig. The reality: channeled with awakened Ryou, the twig's cutting edge extends far beyond its physical length through projected force. Electro arcs along the wood grain. Acidic vapour trails every slash. A chinchilla performing perfect kendō forms with a stick is one of the most dangerous swordsmanship displays in the New World, and the gap between the visual and the output is exactly the kind of thing that makes Rime insufferably happy.

Kenjutsu Mastery

The Chinchilla Who Reinvented the Blade

Rime's swordsmanship is not an imitation of human kenjutsu. It is something entirely new — a philosophy of the blade built from the ground up by a chinchilla who decided the entire history of swordsmanship needed revision and revised it alone in a room lined with acid-scarred walls. His kenjutsu integrates acid-vapour feints, Electro-accelerated slashes, mineral-dust blinds, and Ryou-projected cutting force in combinations no human sword school has ever conceived. Where a human swordsman relies on reach, leverage, and muscle memory, Rime relies on chemical warfare, electrical augmentation, and a Ryou mastery so profound that the twig's cutting edge extends indefinitely through projected force alone.

The result is a swordsmanship that matches the greatest blades in the world. During the engagement against the Red Hair Pirates, Rime parried "Red-Haired" Shanks' Divine Departure — a Conqueror's-coated sword stroke from an Emperor — with his twig. The clash produced a Conqueror's shockwave visible from orbit. For three exchanges, Shanks could not get past him. The man whose swordsmanship is whispered alongside Mihawk's fought a chinchilla with a stick and found a peer, not because the weapons were equal but because Rime's understanding of what a blade could be exceeded anything conventional swordsmanship had prepared Shanks for. Acid trails that punished every parry. Electro-accelerated ripostes faster than muscle memory could counter. Mineral-dust clouds that blinded between slashes. And underneath all of it, Ryou — the deepest, most refined Armament projection on the planet — turning a twelve-centimetre twig into an infinite cutting edge.

Rime teaches his kenjutsu to the Vanguard during training sessions in the Crucible — sessions that started as play and became something extraordinary. The Twelve Who Stand learned to channel Ryou through their tiny weapons with the same philosophy that makes a twig the equal of a legendary blade. Harui's sewing needle. Gankaku's pillow-shield edge. Kozuchi's oversized mallet. All of them became something more under Rime's instruction — not conventional sword techniques adapted for small bodies, but an entirely new martial tradition built by small bodies for small bodies. Rime calls it nothing. He refuses to name his school. But Claude has logged it as the Crucible Tradition, and the Vanguard carry it into every battle.

The Grain Arsenal

Weaponized Hoarding

Rime hoards grain — oats, barley, millet, and whatever cereal he can stockpile with obsessive chinchilla dedication. This is both instinct and deliberate tactical choice. He has modified and infused grain rounds with different effects for battle use. He keeps them in pouches on his tiny bandolier and in hidden stashes all over the Meridian that he thinks nobody knows about (Claude knows about all of them). In combat, he launches them through vapour compression at terrifying velocity, flicks them off the flat of his twig sword with Ryou hardening, or paw-flicks them with Electro charge — his small size means opponents underestimate the lethality of a pudgy chinchilla throwing breakfast cereal until it's far too late. He takes his grain selection as seriously as a sommelier takes wine.

Vapour Rounds
Grain kernels compressed in superheated acidic vapour and launched at high velocity. Explode into scalding corrosive steam clouds on impact. His most common combat projectile. He carries at least thirty at all times.
Electro Rounds
Charged with his Mink Electro. Discharge on contact like miniature lightning grenades. The electrical burst can stun, paralyze, or — at full charge — stop a heart. Rime limits the charge for non-lethal engagements. He does not always limit the charge.
Smoke Rounds
Produce thick concealment fog. Useful for retreats, repositioning, and giving Petal's Observation an advantage while blinding enemy visual tracking. The fog smells faintly of toasted oats, which Rime finds hilarious and everyone else finds oddly comforting in a crisis.
Signal Rounds
Modified by Tidus. Emit a high-pitched frequency that summons the Embers to Rime's location. Used as a rally call. The Embers arrive like a tiny cavalry, disorganized and enthusiastic, which is somehow exactly what Rime needs.
Null Rounds
Rare, difficult to make. Temporarily suppress Devil Fruit abilities in a small radius on detonation. Reverse-engineered from Seastone dust Tidus procured through channels he won't discuss. The suppression lasts only seconds, but in a fight between Devil Fruit users, seconds are lifetimes.
Grain Mines
Scattered across the battlefield, each grain infused with Electro, creating a minefield of tiny explosions. Step on one and the static discharge chains to every other grain in a two-metre radius. A field of breakfast cereal that detonates like a carpet of micro-grenades. Rime considers this his most elegant invention.
Lucky Grain
One golden oat kernel he never throws. He's had it since the lab. He doesn't talk about it. Petal knows what it means. Ajay suspects. It sits in a tiny velvet pouch in the innermost pocket of his bandolier, and when things get truly bad — when the fight seems impossible — Rime touches it once through the fabric, and then he fights like the impossible doesn't apply to him.
The Meridian's Lifeline

Rime's Non-Combat Roles

For all his combat bravado, Rime performs three critical functions aboard the Meridian that make the ship's autonomous operation possible. Without him, the Meridian would need to dock at ports for power, water, and intelligence — three dependencies that a crew this wanted cannot afford.

⚡ Power Generation
Rime's Electro is the reason the Meridian has abundant power despite never docking at ports with recharging capabilities. He generates the ship's electricity — his Mink heritage producing a continuous current that Tidus's engineering routes through the ship's power grid. When the Meridian needs a surge — for weapons, for shields, for Claude's full sensor array — Rime just generates more. The ship runs on the heartbeat of a chinchilla who thinks being a living generator is a perfectly reasonable non-combat hobby. It is, arguably, the most important job on the ship, and Rime treats it with exactly the same casual disregard he treats everything that isn't fighting. He does it flawlessly.
💧 Water Harvesting
The Meridian never runs out of fresh water. This is Rime. His Jōki Jōki no Mi allows him to pull water vapour from the atmosphere — even in arid climates, there is always moisture for a Logia who is vapour. He condenses it into raw water, which Tidus then properly purifies through his water-manipulation abilities, and Petal treats through botanical filtration — a living water treatment process that produces water cleaner than anything a port could supply. The three of them worked out this chain during a drought crossing of the Calm Belt, and it has been standard procedure ever since. Rime finds it boring. He does it anyway, because the crew needs water, and Rime would cross an ocean of boredom for the people on this ship without a second thought.
📡 Radio Wave Relay
Rime's electromagnetic control extends beyond combat applications. By modulating his Electro output into specific frequencies, he acts as a living antenna array — pulling in radio waves, Den Den Mushi transmissions, Marine communications, and electromagnetic signals from across massive distances. This is how Claude accesses information being transmitted around the planet. Rime doesn't decode the signals himself — he simply channels them into Claude's processing architecture, serving as the raw receiver. The result: Claude has access to global communications without any external equipment. Rime is, functionally, the most advanced signals intelligence asset on the Grand Line, and he has absolutely no idea what most of the information he's channeling even means. He doesn't need to. That's Claude's job. Rime's job is to hear everything, and he hears everything.
