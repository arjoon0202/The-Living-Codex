The Meridian's wayfarers — those who came from elsewhere, who chose this ship and were chosen by it — and the Copplings, Tidus's mechanical children, each took the lessons of the Red Storm and made them their own. What follows is the post-engagement training record for the crew members who prove that family is built, not born.

⚙️ COPPLINGS — INTEGRATED COMBAT PROTOCOL

The Copplings — eight mechanical constructs, Dial-core powered, built by Tidus's hands and refined by their own experience — developed something that surprised even their builder. Under Rivet's coordination, they created The Coppling Chain — a formation where all eight link their Dial-core outputs into a synchronized combat network. When the Chain is active, each Coppling can draw on the others' power reserves. Eight individuals become one system with eight bodies.

THE EIGHT — INDIVIDUAL ADVANCEMENT

🔧 RIVET — Field Commander

His wrench now doubles as a kinetic energy weapon — each swing stores momentum, and the release strike carries the accumulated force of every previous blow. Ten swings of warm-up. One swing of reckoning. He has also developed hand signals for the Chain that the other seven read instantly. Rivet doesn't shout orders. He gestures once, and eight Copplings move.

🌱 SPROCKET — Green Barricade

Deploys rapid-growth moss barriers — learned from his time volunteering in Petal's garden. The moss is structural, not decorative. It absorbs kinetic impacts, self-repairs, and grows thicker under stress. Sprocket waters it with his Dial-core coolant. Petal finds this "very sweet." The moss finds it "very nutritious."

🔥 GAUGE — Thermal Vanguard

Heat-resistant vanguard who absorbs and redirects thermal attacks. His Dial-core runs hot by design — Tidus built him for extreme environments. Now Gauge uses that thermal tolerance offensively, walking into fire attacks and emerging with the stored heat channeled into his fists. He punches warm.

🧭 COMPASS — Tactical Geometry

Calculates optimal formation geometry in real-time. During Chain activation, Compass determines where each Coppling needs to be — not where they are, but where they need to be three seconds from now. His navigational precision applied to combat positioning turns the Chain into a constantly optimizing formation.

🩹 PATCH — Combat Medic

Combat medic with Zenmai as force multiplier — the two have become inseparable in battle. Patch repairs biological damage while Zenmai handles mechanical. Between them, there is nothing on the Meridian they cannot fix. Patch has also developed emergency Dial-core transfusion — he can temporarily share his power core output with a damaged Coppling to keep them operational.

⚓ ANVIL — Ceiling Charger

Magnetic hull-walking allows her to attack from impossible angles. She has started charging from the ceiling. Not occasionally — preferentially. Anvil's default combat position is now inverted, running along overhead surfaces and dropping onto targets with gravity-assisted strikes. She finds it more "natural." Everyone else finds it deeply alarming.

🧵 BOBBIN — Barrier Weaver

Defensive specialist who deploys sail-fabric barriers reinforced with her Dial-core energy. The barriers are flexible — they bend under impact rather than shatter, absorbing force across their entire surface. Bobbin can deploy six barriers simultaneously, creating a defensive maze that channels attackers into kill zones. She learned maze theory from Mayoi's maps. The Dustling is flattered.

⚡ SHUTTLE — Rapid Redeployment

The fastest Coppling, and now the Chain's logistics backbone. She carries other Copplings to optimal positions at maximum speed — essentially a combat taxi that ensures the Chain formation can reconfigure in seconds rather than minutes. Shuttle's speed combined with Compass's positioning calculations means the Chain can shift from offensive to defensive geometry faster than most crews can register the change.

🌊 SPECIAL ENTITIES — XYLEM & AKEBONO

🌿 XYLEM — The Conductor

DIAL-CONSTRUCT · BUILT BY TIDUS FOR CLAUDE · SHE/HER

Xylem's post-Mission 17 development centers on her Conqueror's Haki manifestation — the first time a construct entity has ever displayed the Color of the Supreme King. She has been refining it into something she calls The Conductor's Authority — a targeted projection that affects mechanical and construct entities specifically. Under her Authority, Dial-cores resonate, gears align, and ship systems optimize beyond their design parameters. The Meridian performs better when Xylem is confident. Claude monitors this with a mixture of pride and scientific fascination.

🐌 AKEBONO — The Dawn

GIANT SEA SLUG · WARI WARI NO MI · SHE/HER

Akebono's training focused on her Wari Wari no Mi capabilities in coordinated naval combat. She has developed a technique called Dawn Tide — a massive wave-manipulation that repositions the Meridian during ship-to-ship engagements, effectively allowing the crew to choose their engagement angle mid-battle. She also serves as an emergency evacuation platform — her body is large enough to carry the entire crew if the Meridian is compromised. She is very gentle about it. She moves like sunrise: slow, inevitable, and impossible to stop.

🦇 DO-HYUN — THE NIGHT COURIER'S REFINEMENT

BAT · CAESAR PUNK EXPERIMENT #041-D · BUJEOK BUJEOK NO MI · HE/HIM · ₿520,000,000

Do-hyun arrived aboard the Meridian in the aftermath of Mission 17. He watched the Red Storm from two islands away through intercepted communications. He resigned from the Revolutionary courier network, flew to the ship, and asked to stay. He had been aboard for exactly eleven days when he began training — not because anyone asked him to, but because a courier who doesn't improve his routes is a courier who gets intercepted. He brought the same philosophy to combat training: make every path faster, every ward stronger, every second count for more than the last one.

📜 Ward Layering Protocol — 다중부적 (Dajung Bujeok) — Do-hyun's single greatest advancement since joining the crew. Previously, his wards operated independently — one concealment ward, one barrier ward, placed sequentially. Under Rime's Ryou tutelage and Petal's sensory guidance, he learned to layer them. A concealment ward laid over a barrier ward laid over a disruption ward creates a defensive position that is simultaneously invisible, shielded, and disorienting to anyone who breaches the first two layers. Three wards. One surface. One second to deploy. The calligraphy has to be perfect. It always is.

🌀 Echolocation Relay — 반향연결 (Banhyang Yeongyeol) — Do-hyun can now extend his echolocation-Observation fusion through Claude's Mote network, effectively giving him sonar coverage of the entire ship and surrounding waters from any position. When the Mote named Dusk is active near him, his perception range triples. He can map a Marine fleet formation from inside the Meridian's lower deck. He does this most nights, silently, while the crew sleeps. Claude records the data. Do-hyun doesn't ask for credit. Couriers deliver — they don't sign the package.

⚡ Soru-Dive — 급강하 (Geupgangha) — Tidus taught him Soru adapted for bat anatomy — a burst-acceleration technique that converts his natural dive instinct into a combat-grade speed blitz. Do-hyun folds his wings, drops from altitude, and hits Soru at the bottom of the arc, converting gravitational momentum into a horizontal blur that puts him behind an enemy before the enemy registers the sound of wingbeats. The dive is silent. The arrival is not. The Ryou-hardened foot claws make a sound like a stapler punching through steel. Rime described this as "adequate." Do-hyun accepted the compliment.

🛡️ Talisman Armour — 부적갑옷 (Bujeok Gabot) — Do-hyun can now apply barrier wards directly to his own body, creating a form-fitting talisman armour that supplements his leather gear with Haki-reinforced paper plating. Each plate absorbs one significant impact before shattering. He carries enough prepared talismans to armour himself three times over. Replacing shattered plates mid-combat takes him four seconds — he has timed it, because couriers time everything. The armour rustles like paper in the wind. Enemies have mistaken the sound for leaves. The mistake lasts approximately one second.

🗺️ Dead Drop Network — 은닉소 (Eunikso) — A tactical application of his courier training. Do-hyun now establishes concealed supply caches across any battlefield the Meridian engages in — sealed in storage talismans, hidden by concealment wards, each containing emergency medical kits, backup communication equipment, or pre-loaded offensive talismans. He places them during the pre-engagement phase, flying the perimeter while the crew prepares, and marks their positions in a mental map he shares with Claude via the Mote network. The crew has supplies they didn't pack. Do-hyun delivered them before the fight started. That is what couriers do.

🌙 Night Vigil Integration — 야간순찰 (Yagan Sunchal) — Do-hyun's 00:40 Goodnight Round patrol — documented in Claude's Vigil log — has evolved from a simple perimeter check into a full nocturnal surveillance protocol. His echolocation-Haki fusion paints the night sky in three-dimensional sonar while his concealment wards mask the ship from any passing Observation Haki sweeps. He flies the Meridian's perimeter in a precise hexagonal pattern — the same courier route geometry he used for the WENP, adapted for defense instead of delivery. Between Do-hyun's night patrols, Yoru's dream intelligence, Kiri's threshold monitoring, and Claude's Mote field, the Meridian at night is the most comprehensively surveilled vessel on the Grand Line. Nothing approaches without being known. Do-hyun makes sure of it. Then he hangs upside-down from the observation terrace railing and sleeps until noon, his talisman swaying gently in the breeze. The crew has learned not to wake him. Fumble learned this the hard way. Twice.
