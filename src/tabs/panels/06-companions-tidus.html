Self-portrait — chosen by Tidus

🐻‍❄️ Tidus
Heart of the Ocean ・ Vegapunk Experiment #063-C
An adorably chubby polar bear–sea otter hybrid — round-bellied, soft, with dense white-cream fur that carries the water-repellent quality of sea otter pelage over the thick insulating bulk of a polar bear's coat. When wet, his fur takes on an iridescent sheen from the bio-film that coats his body — a gift of his clownfish DNA. Blue eyes, warm and gentle. Slightly oversized paws with subtle webbing between the toes — otter influence that makes him a devastatingly graceful swimmer despite looking like a stuffed animal that wandered into a battlefield. His tail is more otter than bear — flat, broad, useful for swimming and balance. His Pacifista modifications are entirely internal — enhanced skeletal structure, embedded power systems, internal weapon hardpoints — and not visually obvious from outside. Another reason the researchers classified the experiment as a failure. Tidus is the result of Vegapunk attempting to create a fearsome aquatic predator hybrid — polar bear strength, sea otter agility, Fishman DNA, and Pacifista weapons integration. What they got was an overwhelmingly adorable creature whose appearance is the opposite of intimidating.
The World Government wanted a war machine. They got a gentle, brilliant, absurdly lovable bear-otter who happens to possess one of the most powerful wills in recorded history. They threw him away. They were catastrophically wrong.
Tidus is kind in the way that tides are relentless — not as a choice, but as a fundamental property of what he is. He speaks softly, thinks carefully, and treats every living thing he encounters with a gentleness that borders on reverence. He apologizes to furniture he bumps into. He names every homie that finds him and builds them tiny houses nestled in the thick fur on his back. He has never once raised his voice in anger. When asked how he can possess Conqueror's Haki — the embodiment of the will to rule — while being the least aggressive creature anyone has ever met, Tidus simply says: "I don't want to rule anyone. I just want everyone to be safe. Maybe that's enough."
It is more than enough. His Conqueror's Haki is among the strongest ever recorded — and it manifests not as domination but as sanctuary. He is also a genius-level engineer who built the Meridian from salvage and ingenuity and solves mechanical problems the way other people breathe. He is the crew's engineer, medic, craftsman, and emotional anchor — the foundation everything else is built on. Where Ajay designs war, Tidus designs sanctuary. And between the two of them, everyone they love is covered.
His name is his own. As a baby in Vegapunk's lab, he could hear the tides through the walls. The laboratory was coastal — built into cliffs above the sea. At night, when the machines quieted and the researchers went home, the rhythmic pulse of ocean against stone was the only sound that wasn't clinical. It was the only thing in his world that felt like it was there FOR him, not STUDYING him. When he first tried to speak — early, before the researchers expected language — he attempted to say "tides." The word that meant safety. Comfort. The thing that never stopped coming back, no matter how bad the day had been. What came out, in his small, earnest voice, was: "Tidus." He never corrected it. He didn't know it was wrong. The researchers logged it as a designation error — "Subject T-063 self-identifies as 'Tidus,' catalogued as speech development anomaly." They never realized it was a love letter to the only friend he had.
He has a favorite pebble. A smooth, round stone — gray-blue, sea-worn, perfectly ordinary. He keeps it with him always, tucked into the curve of his paw where it fits as though it grew there. He holds it when he thinks. He holds it when he builds. He holds it when the ship is quiet and everyone else is asleep and the ocean is dark beneath them. No one knows where it came from. Tidus has never explained, and no one has ever asked, because some things don't need explaining. They just need holding.
Personality expressions: When content, he chuffs softly and rolls onto his back, eyes closing — otter grooming behavior at its most peaceful. When excited about engineering, his paws patter rapidly on whatever surface is nearest, tail smacking the ground in bear-cub excitement. In protective mode, he rises to full height on hind legs — a polar bear's threat display from the gentlest creature alive — with a low rumbling purr and fur bristling slightly. When alarmed, a sharp bark; teeth bared (formidable polar bear teeth), positioning himself between threat and whoever needs protecting. When happy, a cooing chirp — sea otter influence — and sometimes he floats on his back in water and chirps, which is his most peaceful state. When apologetic (which is often, especially after hitting opponents), he ducks his head, covers his muzzle with both paws, and makes small whimpering sounds. He does this after every fight. Every single one.

Haki Profile

Tidus' Haki Mastery

Tidus' Haki profile is one of the most unusual in the world. He has no interest in fighting, no bloodlust, no ambition for conquest. He is, by every measure, a gentle soul. And yet his Conqueror's Haki is among the most powerful ever manifested — a paradox that confounds everyone who encounters it. His will is not the will to dominate. It is the will to protect absolutely, and that conviction is so total, so unwavering, that reality itself yields to it.

Conqueror's Haki
Supreme Ruler
Advanced Coating — Full Infusion Mastery
Among the most powerful Conqueror's Haki ever recorded — superior to "Red-Haired" Shanks'. During the Egghead engagement, Tidus' domain-level Conqueror's pushed Shanks' will back for the first time in the Emperor's adult life, forcing his domain to contract. Has achieved full advanced coating — can imbue Conqueror's into his defenses at extreme concentration, release it through Fishman Karate techniques, and project sovereign will through everything he creates. His Conqueror's does not oppress — it shelters. It is the will to protect absolutely, and that conviction is so total that even an Emperor's authority becomes a visitor within Tidus' jurisdiction, not a resident.

Fishman Heritage

Fishman DNA — Clownfish + Fancy Goldfish

Among the DNA spliced into Experiment #063-C, the Fishman components were selected for aquatic adaptation and underwater combat capability. What Vegapunk got was something far more interesting than a weapon.

Clownfish (Amphiprioninae): Tidus produces a protective bio-film coating that grants immunity to contact toxins, acids, and chemical attacks — the real-world superpower of clownfish, who produce a film membrane that makes them immune to anemone stings. Tidus can extend this bio-film to coat allies, creating incredible synergy with Rime's acidic vapor — he is naturally immune to it and can protect others walking through acid clouds. The clownfish DNA also gives him a fierce territorial instinct: despite being gentle, he gets aggressive for everyone he considers family. His symbiotic nature means he doesn't just protect — he enables. His presence makes everyone around him stronger.

Fancy Goldfish (Ranchu/Oranda type): Goldfish survive conditions that kill most fish — temperature extremes, low oxygen, poor water quality. The goldfish DNA is why Tidus is indestructible despite looking like a plush toy. It contributes to his adorable roundness, enhances his bio-film production, and embodies his core aesthetic: looks harmless, survives everything. Peak Tidus energy.

Combat Sound Signature: A deep resonant POP — the sound of film-coated water bursting under enormous pressure. Think of a giant bubble popping with concussive force. When the crew hears that sound, they know Tidus just hit something with intent.

Martial Art

Fishman Karate — Grandmaster (Adapted)

Tidus' Fishman Karate has reached grandmaster-level proficiency — adapted entirely for his bear-otter anatomy. His fighting school is built around film-membrane water manipulation — thick, viscous water constructs that aren't just walls but sticky, clinging, shock-absorbing membranes. His bio-film coating from the clownfish DNA gives his water constructs an iridescent quality and a texture that no other Fishman Karate practitioner can replicate — elastic, adhesive, shimmering.

His primary striking technique is the Paw-Clap Shockwave — Tidus claps his heavy polar bear paws together, compressing film-coated water between them. The resulting pop sends a viscous shockwave that clings to whatever it hits. Unlike a sharp impact that pushes through, this shockwave wraps around the target and continues delivering internal pressure damage after the initial hit. Less sharp crack, more deep resonant pop. His blocks create sticky water barriers that grab and slow incoming attacks. His strikes deliver force through a film-water medium that adheres to the target. Underwater, he's even more devastating — polar bear swimming power plus otter agility plus Fishman water control.

Martial Arts

Rokushiki — Advanced Operative

Tidus has learned Rokushiki to an advanced operative level — the tier associated with the World Government's most elite intelligence agents. Each art adapted for his polar bear-otter body:

Tekkai: Devastating given his natural bulk — dense polar bear muscle, thick fur, Pacifista skeletal reinforcement, and bio-film coating. Combined with Conqueror's Haki, he becomes a fortress that not even cannon fire can scratch. The most natural Rokushiki for Tidus — he was built to take hits.

Geppo: Powerful hind-leg bursts — bears have incredible leg strength. Less rapid than Rime's, but each air-step covers more distance and hits harder. The air beneath him compresses visibly. His Geppo looks like a polar bear bounding through the sky.

Soru: Forward charges rather than lateral movement. A polar bear at Soru-speed is a freight train of fluff. The ground cracks. The air displaces. And then the softest possible collision.

Shigan: Paw-thrust variant. Less pointed than finger-pistol — bears have blunt paws — more about channeling massive force through a single point. Also: his bite. Polar bear jaw strength is extraordinary.

Rankyaku: Comes from tail sweeps — his otter tail is flat and powerful — or massive paw swipes that compress air into cutting waves. The waves are thick and visible, like water blades.

Kami-e: The most surprising one. Tidus looks like he shouldn't be able to dodge anything. But sea otters are incredibly fluid in water, and that translates to a deceptive body fluidity on land. His Kami-e is a gentle, swaying evasion — opponents' attacks slide off him like he's made of water.
