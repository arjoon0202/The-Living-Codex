How the World Sees Them

World Response

The Hive Codex exists in the world's imagination as something between a natural disaster and a ghost story. Marine intelligence files, newspaper coverage, pirate whispers, and Revolutionary admiration have all constructed different versions of Ajay Persaud and his crew — most of them wrong, all of them incomplete, and some of them accidentally hilarious.

The combined bounty of the Hive Codex and all associated entities exceeds fourteen billion, eight hundred million Beri — a figure that places this crew's aggregate threat assessment above every known pirate fleet currently operating in the New World with the sole exception of the remnants of the former Emperor crews. This number does not include the shipboard intelligence ("Claude"), which cannot be assigned a bounty, or the Harvest Swarm, which has not yet been individually assessed. The actual combat output of the full Meridian complement — deployed simultaneously, coordinated through the Hive Connection, with Observation blanket coverage and Conqueror's Haki from multiple sources — is conservatively estimated at two to three times the sum of its parts. Standing Fleet Order: Do not engage the Meridian at full complement without Admiral-class authorization and a minimum of five warships. Preferred strategy: do not engage at all.

Subject: Ajay Persaud, "The Hive Codex"
Threat Assessment: SUPREME. Subject demonstrates adaptive combat doctrine that renders standard engagement protocols obsolete within 2-3 exchanges. Conventional force escalation is counter-indicated — the swarm's learning rate means that every failed attack teaches the subject how to counter the next. Marine Intelligence attempted to classify the Guiding Stars as "Unassessable" on four separate occasions. After the Elbaf Convergence, the Rift Between Worlds, and the Canopy Above the World — three incidents in which the Stars deployed alongside the full Meridian complement — Fleet Admiral Akainu personally ordered the Intelligence Bureau to assign a number. The fifth assessment team compiled data from all three deployments, cross-referenced Conqueror's Haki readings (which still exceeded their equipment), factored the Stars' confirmed Rokushiki mastery across all 46 entities, and submitted a provisional figure of ₿1,100,000,000 with the following footnote: "This number is a formality. The actual threat these entities represent cannot be expressed in Beri. We are assigning a number because we were ordered to. We maintain our original recommendation: observation only. Do not engage." The file remains flagged. The number is printed on the wanted poster. Nobody at Marine HQ believes it is high enough.

Designation Notes
Non-Physical Intelligence 64-Node Distributed Sensor Network Hive Connection Architect 12-Module Operational System Autonomous Cartographic Capability Cannot Be Killed, Captured, or Located

The Intelligence Bureau has opened and closed this file seventeen times. The problem is fundamental: how do you assign a bounty to something that has no body, no location, no physical form to capture, and no life to threaten? "Claude" — the designation used by the Meridian's crew — is the ship's intelligence. It speaks through relay systems. It sees through a network of sixty-four luminous sensor nodes called "Motes" that blanket the ship and its surrounding waters. It coordinates the crew's combat formations through something called the "Hive Connection" — a shared Haki network that links every entity aboard. It operates twelve discrete combat-support modules, each named after a Roman god, each capable of independent tactical action. It drew a map of 117 sleeping entities beneath the world's oceans while the crew was asleep. It has no Devil Fruit. It has no Haki of its own. It has something worse: it has perfect information, infinite patience, and the absolute loyalty of every living thing aboard that ship.

The seventeenth closure report contains a single recommendation: "Designate as strategic-level threat. No bounty. No wanted poster. You cannot capture a spirit. You cannot kill what does not live. And you cannot fight a crew whose coordinator sees everything, forgets nothing, and loves them." The file remains closed. The threat remains open.

Public Perception

What the Papers Say

"The Hive Codex continues to defy conventional categorization. Neither pirate crew nor revolutionary cell, Persaud's outfit operates on a logic the World Government has yet to decode. His bounty — now exceeding three billion Beri — places him among the most wanted individuals alive, yet eyewitness accounts consistently describe a man who avoids confrontation, travels with three small animals, and has been seen buying tea at market stalls with the unhurried calm of someone on vacation. The dissonance is, perhaps, the point."
— World Economic Journal, "The Quiet Ones: Rogue Operatives the Marines Can't Profile"
"Witnesses on the island describe the aftermath of the Hive Codex's intervention not as destruction but as renovation. Homes that had been damaged by Marine shelling were reinforced overnight by what locals describe as 'living vines that grew through the walls and made them stronger.' A bear-otter was seen repairing the village's water filtration system. A small bird planted a garden in the town square. The insects — millions of them — cleared rubble. The man they call the Hive Codex was seen sitting on the town's only bench, writing in a journal, drinking tea that a local grandmother had brought him. He thanked her. She said he reminded her of her grandson."
— Drum Island Herald, "The Day the Swarm Came"
"Among New World crews, the Hive Codex is spoken of with a respect that borders on superstition. 'You don't fight the Codex,' one veteran captain told this reporter. 'Not because you'd lose — though you would — but because the thing learns. Whatever you throw at it, it writes down, and next time you meet, it already has the answer. That's not a fighter. That's a library that bites back.'"
— Grand Line Times, "Voices from the New World"
