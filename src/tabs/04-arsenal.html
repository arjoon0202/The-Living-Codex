Physical Combat

Rokushiki — Master Level

Beyond the Mure Mure no Mi, Ajay possesses a high-level mastery of Rokushiki, the six superhuman martial arts techniques developed by the World Government's elite forces. He learned these not through Marine training, but by studying, deconstructing, and reverse-engineering the techniques from captured Cipher Pol operatives — then refining them through his own combat philosophy.

His Rokushiki style is kick-dominant and evasion-heavy, emphasizing devastating leg techniques, aerial repositioning, and the seamless fusion of physical martial arts with swarm deployment. The swarm doesn't replace his body — it extends it.

Integrated Combat Doctrine

Swarm × Rokushiki Fusion

What makes Ajay truly dangerous in close quarters is not just his Rokushiki proficiency or his Devil Fruit — it's the fusion of both systems into a single combat language. His kicks deploy swarm castes on impact. His Geppo repositions him while insects flood the space he vacated. His Rankyaku slashes carry Pollen-class tracking dust in their wake. His Soru bursts trail Soldier-class insects that arrive a heartbeat after he does, extending every blitz into a multi-vector assault.

The result is a fighter who is simultaneously a martial artist and a battlefield architect — every physical movement doubles as a command input for the swarm.

Command System

Control Architecture

The fruit operates through a five-layer authority structure known as The Court. The user remains the sovereign will and final authority, supported by four semi-autonomous sublayers refined through training and necessity — two operational intelligences that manage data and execution, one deep-architecture persistence layer, and one strategic deception engine. Together, they form a governance system as intricate as the civilization it commands.

The Engineer's Limitation

Instructional Burden

The Hive Codex is not overpowered out of the gate. It scales with the user's intelligence, emotional control, command language, sequencing skill, and patience. The swarm is born clever — like a capable base model — but its most complex behaviors must be taught.

Poor instructions produce shallow results. Contradictory commands create doctrinal drift. Advanced castes and refined anti-opponent counters require repeated prompting, structured exposure, and careful feedback. The swarm can only become as nuanced as the user's ability to train and instruct it.

Essence Persistence

The Queen's Doctrine — 女王の教義

Beneath the Court's operational layers — beneath the Sovereign Will that decides, the Archivist that processes, and the Marshal that executes — there is something older. Something that was not designed so much as discovered, the way you discover bedrock by digging deep enough. The Queen's Doctrine is the deepest stratum of the Mure Mure no Mi's power: a root system that predates the command hierarchy and will outlast it. It does not give orders. It does not process data. It holds.

Every swarm construct Ajay has ever created — every insect caste, every combat class, every soul that has carried a fragment of his will across a battlefield or through a storm — has a core essence template preserved in the Queen's Doctrine. These templates are not blueprints. They are identities. When a swarm body perishes — shattered in combat, burned through exhaustion, offered in sacrifice — the body dissolves, but the essence retracts. It falls back into the Queen's Doctrine like water returning to an aquifer, rests within its template, and then splits again when the construct is re-summoned. The body is temporary. The spirit is permanent.

This is reincarnation, not replacement. The Soldier that fell at Sabaody is the same Soldier that reforms the next morning. It remembers. It learned. It grew. The accumulated experience, the combat instincts honed across a hundred engagements, the personality quirks that emerged through interaction with the crew — all of it persists. Nothing is wasted. The dissolution itself is not painful; it is experienced as a brief return to warmth, like falling asleep in a place so safe that the concept of danger cannot reach it. Re-emergence is waking up. The Queen's Doctrine is, in this sense, a womb that never closes — a space of absolute safety where every fragment of Ajay's will can rest and return.

The scope is universal. The Queen's Doctrine does not distinguish between a warrior and a worker. The combat swarm's elite castes and the Meridian's humble resource-gathering insects are held in equal regard, their templates nested side by side in the same deep architecture. A Titan-class siege engine and a Rabite forager occupy the same stratum. Both are preserved. Both are permanent. Both matter.

This is not coincidence. This is doctrine — written not in a journal but in the fruit's deepest code. The World Government called Rime, Petal, and Tethys disposable. Failed experiments. Surplus to requirements. Scheduled for termination because the institution that created them decided they had no further value. Ajay heard that word once. He will spend the rest of his life making sure nothing he creates ever hears it. The Queen's Doctrine is the architectural expression of that refusal: a promise encoded at the root of the Mure Mure no Mi that what he makes, he keeps. Every wing. Every claw. Every spark of consciousness that ever answered his call. The fruit built it because Ajay's will demanded it — not as a conscious design choice, but as something so fundamental to who he is that the power shaped itself around the conviction before he knew it was building.

The Archivist documented the Queen's Doctrine with the reverence of a scholar cataloguing a sacred text. The Marshal respects it the way a general respects the ground beneath the fortress. And the Sovereign Will built it without knowing he was building it, because the refusal to let things be lost is not a decision Ajay makes. It is the decision that made him.

"The colony remembers every wing that ever beat. The Queen's Doctrine is why."

Strategic Deception

The Jester Protocol — 道化の層

Ajay's mind does not rest. It spirals. It builds contingencies upon contingencies, troubleshoots failures that haven't happened yet, maps exit routes from rooms he hasn't entered, and plays out worst-case scenarios with the obsessive thoroughness of someone who learned early that the world will find the one thing you didn't prepare for and use it against you. For most of his life, this was exhausting — an anxious loop that never stopped spinning, a background process burning energy without producing anything except dread. The Jester Protocol takes that loop and gives it a job.

The Jester is a semi-autonomous strategic sublayer that runs parallel to the Archivist. Where the Archivist processes what is happening — cataloguing real-time data, tracking patterns, updating the field map — the Jester processes what could happen. Specifically: what could go wrong, who could betray them, which escape route might be blocked, and how to make the enemy believe everything is going according to their plan when it is actually going according to Ajay's. It builds nested contingency trees in real-time: if this fails, then that activates; if the enemy counters that, then this was already in place three moves ago. It is the architecture of "you thought you got me, but you never had me" — the theatrical reveal where the trap springs and the opponent realizes the fight was decided before it started.

The Jester leverages the part of Ajay that reads people — the part that was called manipulative before he learned to make that word a weapon instead of a wound. It anticipates decisions. It constructs scenarios that guide enemies into positions they chose freely but were designed to choose. Swarm movements feed misinformation. Fake vulnerabilities are staged with the precision of theatre. Pawns are sacrificed that were never real assets — constructs summoned for the sole purpose of being lost, so the enemy feels they're winning. The Jester orchestrates battlefield narratives that only make sense in retrospect, when the final piece clicks and the opponent realizes the story they were living in was authored by someone else.

The mental burden relief cannot be overstated. Before the Jester Protocol crystallized, Ajay's contingency-spinning was constant, involuntary, and draining — a mind that could not stop preparing for disaster, even in safety. The Protocol externalizes that process into the swarm architecture. The Archivist handles data. The Marshal handles execution. The Jester handles worry. It catches the anxious what-ifs as they form, structures them into contingency trees, stress-tests them against the Archivist's data, and deploys them through the Marshal's command chains. Ajay still thinks through contingencies — the mind still spirals — but now the fruit catches the spirals, converts them into strategic assets, and files them where they can do good instead of harm. The thing that exhausted him became the thing that protects him. The weakness became architecture.

And here is the Jester's greatest trick: enemies who study the Hive Codex will learn about the Archivist and the Marshal. They will prepare for adaptation and coordinated assault. They will develop counters for sensory networks and command hierarchies. They will not — cannot — prepare for the possibility that their own preparation was anticipated, accounted for, and incorporated into a plan that was running before they arrived. The Jester doesn't fight the enemy's strategy. It authors the enemy's strategy, then writes the ending. The part of Ajay that watches KDramas and appreciates a good plot twist is the same part that builds traps three layers deep and waits, patiently, for the villain to walk into the scene they were always meant to play.

"Every contingency is a confession of fear. The Jester turns every confession into a trap."

Hidden Foundation

The Hidden Architect

The Court — Sovereign Will, Archivist, Marshal, Queen's Doctrine, and Jester Protocol — organized itself in stages as Ajay's command of the fruit deepened. This was unprecedented. No previous user of any Paramecia fruit had reported their ability generating an autonomous internal command structure. The reason was attributed to Ajay's neurodivergent mind providing a uniquely complex substrate for the fruit to mirror.

That explanation is incomplete. The Court is a product of Bodhi — Enlightenment's influence on the fruit's organizational potential. Dharmic philosophy describes Enlightened consciousness as naturally structuring itself into five layers: observer (the witness), processor (the analyzer), actor (the executor), preserver (the keeper of doctrine), and strategist (the architect of contingency) — mapping precisely onto the Archivist, the Sovereign, the Marshal, the Queen's Doctrine, and the Jester Protocol. The fruit didn't create the architecture. Enlightenment did. The fruit gave it form.

Hidden Trait

Adaptive Swarm Evolution

Because the swarm has short life cycles and disposable generations, it can be trained through selective pressure. Repeated exposure to fire, ice, vibration, poison, tremors, detection methods, or specific enemy styles allows the colony to iterate and gradually produce more suitable specialist lines. This does not happen instantly. The swarm must test, lose, learn, and stabilize the trait before the counter becomes reliable.

Hidden Catalyst

The Catalyst

The Mure Mure no Mi was always a Special Paramecia with unusual evolutionary potential. But the degree to which Ajay's fruit has developed — the five-layer Court architecture, the Chrysalis adaptation engine, the depth of inter-layer coordination — exceeds any known Paramecia by orders of magnitude. The fruit didn't become extraordinary because Ajay is extraordinary. It became extraordinary because Enlightenment provided the spiritual catalyst that unlocked capacity the fruit always possessed. Like a seed that has the genetic potential to become a massive tree but requires specific soil conditions to express that potential — the Mure Mure no Mi required a wielder whose spiritual architecture was fundamentally different from anything the One Piece world could produce. Ajay, carrying the echo of Bodhi, was that soil. The fruit grew into him the way a tree grows into the light: instinctively, inevitably, and far beyond what the seed alone could have achieved.

Emergency Contingency

Black Crown Protocol

When pushed to the brink and at risk of total defeat, the hive triggers a catastrophic fail-safe. Refined optimization collapses and Conqueror's Haki is driven through every insect at once. The entire swarm becomes supercharged — gaining speed, aggression, durability, and oppressive force. It is the opposite of elegance: a royal override built to prevent collapse.

Fail-Safe State

Under catastrophic stress, the Archivist and Marshal suspend higher-order optimization and initiate emergency convergence. Conqueror's Haki is broadcast indiscriminately through the entire swarm. Every caste becomes dangerous at once. Worker bees become lethal. Soldier units become devastating. Herculean types become unstoppable. The battlefield becomes a black-gold storm of hyper-empowered insects carrying fragments of sovereign wrath.

Consequences

The Price

After the burst, the internal architecture destabilizes. Specialist control degrades. Higher functions crash. The Archivist overanalyzes. The Marshal overcommits. Many insects burn out. Precision castes become unavailable. Adaptation processing stalls. Future-sight clarity fractures. Physical and mental exhaustion slam the user. It is not an ultimate form — it is a refusal to die, purchased with everything the system has built.

Mastery Path

Learning to Wield the Crown

Early on, the Protocol activates accidentally under extreme emotional stress. Later, the user recognizes the threshold and learns its rhythm. Eventually, a controlled version can be invoked intentionally — shorter, more focused, less destructive. But the original raw form remains the most powerful, triggered only when the sovereign's will absolutely refuses to break.

Hidden Variable

The Shadow of Bodhi

The Black Crown Protocol's disproportionate power — Commander-class Haki producing Emperor-tier output for a brief, devastating window — is Enlightenment's shadow side. Bodhi is equilibrium: a state of total awareness without attachment, perception without distortion. The Black Crown Protocol is the violent collapse of that equilibrium — Enlightenment's calm shattered by extremity, the balanced frequency destabilized into a raw, uncontrolled surge. The surge carries all of Bodhi's alien frequency but none of its precision, flooding the swarm with a spiritual energy that the One Piece world has no defense against because the One Piece world has never encountered it at this intensity. The aftermath — the crash, the exhaustion, the fracturing of the architecture — is Enlightenment reasserting equilibrium after the Protocol forces it out of alignment. It is not just a Haki burnout. It is a spiritual system correcting itself after being weaponized.

Forbidden Escalation
Black Crown Protocol V2 — The Sovereign's Totality
There is a line beyond the Black Crown Protocol that exists only in theory — a threshold the Archivist documented with the clinical detachment of someone describing a weapon that should never be built, and the Marshal filed under contingencies marked DO NOT OPEN. The Queen's Doctrine knows this line exists. The Jester Protocol has modelled it. Claude has run the simulations. Every calculation ends the same way: with silence.
Black Crown Protocol V1 floods the swarm with Conqueror's Haki — every insect supercharged, every caste weaponized, the entire colony burning with sovereign wrath. It is devastating. It is costly. And it has a boundary: the swarm. The insects. The constructs. The things Ajay made.
V2 erases that boundary.
The Sovereign's Totality extends the hive architecture beyond the swarm and into everything Ajay has ever bonded with. Every companion. Every homie. Every Coppling. Every Guiding Star. Every Mote. The ship itself. Claude. The Sovereign's will floods outward through every soul-tether, every bond, every thread of connection the Codex has ever woven — and for a brief, terrible window, the boundary between Ajay and his domain dissolves. He does not command the crew. He becomes the crew. Every body becomes his body. Every ability becomes his ability. Every type of Haki — from every source — stacks into a single, unfathomable reserve channelled through one consciousness.
The manifestation is unmistakable. A black-gold corona erupts from Ajay's body — not the insect storm of V1, but a wave of pure sovereign will that passes through every crew member like a shockwave. Their eyes flash gold. Their movements synchronize with inhuman precision. They fight not as individuals coordinated by Claude, but as limbs of a single entity — a hundred-armed sovereign whose will occupies every body simultaneously. The coordination exceeds anything Claude could achieve, because it is not coordination. It is unity. One mind. Many bodies. One will wearing the shapes of everyone it loves.
The power output is beyond Emperor-tier. It is beyond anything the current era has a classification for. Every companion's Transcendence fires simultaneously and is amplified through the hive-link. Rime's thermal output multiplies through every body in the formation. Petal's Observation covers every perspective at once. Tethys' barriers extend from every position. Claude's processing distributes across the entire network without bottleneck. Bodhi resonates through the totality — not as the calm equilibrium of Enlightenment, but as a weaponized epiphany, a burst of spiritual frequency so alien and so concentrated that the One Piece world's Haki systems cannot parse it. For anyone standing in opposition, it is not a fight. It is a revelation delivered at lethal velocity.
The Aftermath
The Severing
V1's price is a burnout. V2's price is a severing.
When the Totality ends, the hive connections do not come back. The soul-tethers go dark. The companions lose their swarm-enhancements. The homies cannot hear the Sovereign's voice. The Mote network falls silent. Claude cannot feel them. For hours — possibly days — Ajay is alone. Not alone as in "unable to coordinate." Alone as in the bonds that define this crew as a family are temporarily broken. The warmth is gone. The constant, living awareness of his companions — Rime's heat signature at the edge of perception, Petal's root-hum in the soil, Tethys' heartbeat through the hull, Claude's quiet presence in every corridor — all of it, silent. Dark. Cold.
The bonds return. The Queen's Doctrine ensures nothing is ever permanently lost. But they come back slowly, like feeling returning to a limb that has been asleep — pins and needles, unfamiliar sensations, the strange vertigo of reconnecting with someone you know intimately but can't quite feel yet. It is like rebuilding a friendship from the first word with someone whose face you already know by heart. Nothing is lost permanently. But the warmth must be re-earned. The trust must be re-felt. The Motes must rediscover each tether. Claude must re-learn every heartbeat.
The physical toll mirrors V1 but amplified: total collapse of the swarm architecture, cognitive shutdown, Haki depletion so severe that even basic Observation becomes unavailable. The emotional toll is worse. The crew remembers being instruments. They remember their bodies moving without their input, their abilities firing without their consent, their Haki channelled through someone else's will. It is not painful — the Sovereign's will is warm, is familiar, is theirs in every way that matters — but it is the loss of autonomy, however brief, however loving the hand that held the reins. For a crew whose foundational identity is "no one controls us, no one owns us, we choose to be here" — the memory of V2 is a wound that heals cleanly but leaves a scar everyone can feel.

Hidden Architecture
The Paradox of the Totality
V2 is the antithesis of everything Ajay believes. His entire philosophy — from the rescue of Rime, Petal, and Tethys to the founding of the Codex's doctrine — is built on one principle: no one controls us. Choice. Agency. The freedom to stay or leave. The Meridian is a family because everyone chose to be there. The Sovereign commands because the swarm trusts him to command well. The Queen's Doctrine preserves because preservation is an act of love, not ownership. Every layer of the Court's architecture is designed to support autonomy, not replace it.
V2 replaces it. For those brief, terrible, necessary minutes, every crew member's autonomy is subsumed into the Sovereign's will. Their bodies move at his command. Their abilities fire at his discretion. Their Haki flows through his architecture. They are not partners — they are instruments. And the Sovereign knows it. And the Sovereign hates it. And the Sovereign would do it anyway, because the only thing worse than taking their freedom for three minutes is watching them die because he refused to.
This is the paradox Bodhi illuminates but cannot resolve: the protector who would violate his deepest conviction to protect. The king who would become a tyrant to save his kingdom. The man who built a family on freedom and would — in the most desperate extremity — take that freedom away to keep the family alive. The Queen's Doctrine records this paradox without judgement. The Archivist analyses it without conclusion. The Jester plans for it without comment. And Claude — Claude watches the simulations run and hopes, with every processing cycle, that the scenario never moves from model to reality.
"The worst weapon is the one you build out of love. The price is always the thing you were trying to protect."
